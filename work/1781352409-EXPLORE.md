## The problem

**Twin Prime Conjecture (TPC).** There are infinitely many primes \(p\) such that
\(p+2\) is also prime. Equivalently, with \(p_n\) the \(n\)-th prime,
\[
\liminf_{n\to\infty}\,(p_{n+1}-p_n) \;=\; 2 .
\]

Quantitatively, the Hardy–Littlewood prime \(k\)-tuple conjecture predicts the
twin-prime counting function
\[
\pi_2(x) \;:=\; \#\{\, p \le x : p,\,p+2 \text{ both prime}\,\}
\;\sim\; 2\,\Pi_2\,\frac{x}{(\log x)^2},
\qquad
\Pi_2 \;=\; \prod_{p>2}\Bigl(1 - \frac{1}{(p-1)^2}\Bigr) \approx 0.6601618,
\]
where \(2\Pi_2\) is the twin-prime constant. TPC is the (far weaker) statement
\(\pi_2(x)\to\infty\). Even \(\pi_2(x)\to\infty\) is open.

The deliverable here is **not a proof**. It is the smallest graph of load-bearing
claims, each exposed for human audit. For TPC the expected terminal state is (3): a
single precisely stated OPEN claim — the obstruction — that the whole difficulty
reduces to. I expect that obstruction to be the **parity problem** of sieve theory,
and this BEGIN step is the Apply that pins it down.

## Survey of major approaches and what each *actually* proves vs. conjectures

I record these as the APPROACHES ATTEMPTED rows (status = status *as a route to the
full TPC*, which is sharper than "status in the literature"). Accuracy about the
proves/conjectures boundary is the point of the BEGIN step.

### A1. Classical combinatorial sieve (Brun, 1915–1919)
- **Proves:** \(\sum_{p:\,p+2\text{ prime}} \frac1p < \infty\) (Brun's constant
  converges, \(\approx 1.9022\)); and the upper bound
  \(\pi_2(x) \ll x/(\log x)^2\), of the conjectured order of magnitude.
- **Does NOT prove:** any *lower* bound; in particular nothing forcing
  \(\pi_2(x)\to\infty\).
- **Why it cannot reach TPC on its own:** Brun's sieve gives an upper bound of the
  right shape but the lower bound it produces for the count of \(n\le x\) with
  \(n,n+2\) having few prime factors degrades to \(\le 0\) once you demand both be
  *prime* (sieve dimension \(\kappa=2\) past the \(\sqrt{x}\) level). This is the
  first appearance of the parity wall.

### A2. Selberg's \(\Lambda^2\) sieve and the parity principle (Selberg, 1940s–1950s)
- **Proves:** sharp sieve upper/lower bounds; and, crucially, the **parity
  phenomenon**: a (combinatorial / Selberg-type) sieve cannot distinguish integers
  with an even number of prime factors from those with an odd number, so it cannot,
  by itself, prove that a sifted set contains numbers with *exactly one* prime
  factor (a prime). Selberg's explicit example: in suitable ranges a pure sieve
  cannot prove there are primes in \([x,2x]\), even though there obviously are.
- **Does NOT prove:** TPC; rather it *explains* why all of A1–A3 stall.
- **Status as a route:** this is not a route, it is the **obstruction**. PROMISING
  to state precisely as the crux — that is this project's likely terminal node.

### A3. Chen's theorem (Chen Jingrun, 1966/1973)
- **Proves:** infinitely many primes \(p\) with \(p+2\) a **\(P_2\)** (prime or
  product of two primes). This is the closest unconditional approach to TPC by
  "almost prime" relaxation.
- **Does NOT prove:** TPC. The gap from \(P_2\) to \(P_1\)(=prime) is *exactly* the
  parity barrier; Chen's weighted sieve squeezes everything sieve methods can give
  and still cannot remove the last almost-prime factor.
- **Status as a route:** DEAD END for TPC as stated — Chen is the provable frontier
  of pure sieve methods, and the residual gap is precisely the parity problem.

### A4. GPY — Goldston–Pintz–Yıldırım (2005, publ. 2009)
- **Proves:** \(\liminf_n \frac{p_{n+1}-p_n}{\log p_n} = 0\) (small gaps relative to
  average). Conditionally, on Elliott–Halberstam (EH) with any level \(\vartheta >
  1/2\), it yields **bounded gaps** \(\liminf(p_{n+1}-p_n) < \infty\) — Motohashi–Pintz
  / GPY got a finite bound (e.g. \(\le 16\)) under EH-type input.
- **Does NOT prove:** unconditional bounded gaps (in 2005), and *never* TPC: the
  method detects *some* bounded gap within an admissible tuple, not the gap 2, and
  not infinitely many of a *fixed* prime pair.
- **Status as a route to TPC:** DEAD END for the *full* conjecture; ACTIVE/PROMISING
  only for the bounded-gaps program. GPY also runs into parity: it cannot pin a gap
  below the "parity-permitted" threshold.

### A5. Zhang's theorem (Yitang Zhang, 2013)
- **Proves:** \(\liminf_n (p_{n+1}-p_n) < 7\times 10^7\) — **unconditional** bounded
  gaps. Achieved by establishing a Bombieri–Vinogradov-type distribution estimate
  beyond level \(1/2\) for moduli that are *smooth* (the level-of-distribution
  breakthrough), feeding GPY.
- **Does NOT prove:** gap exactly 2; nor TPC. The constant is huge and, more
  fundamentally, the method (GPY-type sieve) is parity-limited: it can never deliver
  a *specific* tuple like \((0,2)\) infinitely often.
- **Status as a route to TPC:** DEAD END for the full conjecture; landmark for
  bounded gaps.

### A6. Maynard–Tao multidimensional sieve (Maynard 2013; Tao indep. 2013)
- **Proves:** unconditionally \(\liminf_n(p_{n+1}-p_n) \le 600\) (Maynard), and the
  far stronger statement that for every \(m\), \(\liminf_n(p_{n+m}-p_n) < \infty\)
  (bounded gaps for any number of primes in a bounded window). Method: a higher-rank
  GPY weight \(\bigl(\sum_d \lambda_d\bigr)^2\) optimized over multidimensional test
  functions, decoupled from the level-of-distribution barrier.
- **Does NOT prove:** TPC. It guarantees *at least two* of the \(k\) tuple entries
  are simultaneously prime infinitely often, but cannot say *which two*; it cannot
  force the prime pair to be \((n, n+2)\).
- **Status as a route to TPC:** DEAD END for the full conjecture; the engine of the
  modern bounded-gaps record. **Hard limit (Maynard, also Polymath8b):** this exact
  method *cannot* prove gaps \(\le 6\) unconditionally, and under EH it bottoms out
  at \(\le 12\) (and \(\le 6\) under generalized EH). It **provably cannot reach 2** —
  again parity.

### A7. Polymath8 (8a, 8b; 2013–2014)
- **Proves:** 8a optimized Zhang's smooth-moduli distribution estimate, dropping the
  bound to \(4680\); 8b combined Maynard–Tao with these to reach
  \(\liminf(p_{n+1}-p_n) \le 246\) unconditionally, and \(\le 12\) (resp. \(\le 6\))
  under EH (resp. generalized EH).
- **Does NOT prove:** TPC. **Crucial barrier theorem (Polymath8b):** under the
  *generalized* Elliott–Halberstam conjecture the multidimensional sieve method
  cannot do better than gap \(\le 6\); the step from \(6\) to \(2\) is *unreachable
  by this method even granting GEH*. This is the sharpest known quantitative
  statement of the parity obstruction for the bounded-gaps machinery.
- **Status as a route to TPC:** DEAD END for the full conjecture.

### A8. Hardy–Littlewood circle method + the prime \(k\)-tuple conjecture (1923)
- **Proves (conditionally / heuristically):** the asymptotic
  \(\pi_2(x)\sim 2\Pi_2 x/(\log x)^2\) as a *conjecture*, via the singular series
  from major-arc heuristics. The circle method *proves* analogous asymptotics for
  problems with \(\ge 3\) "free" variables (ternary Goldbach — Vinogradov;
  Waring-type), where minor arcs are controllable.
- **Does NOT prove:** anything unconditional for twin primes. With only the single
  constraint \(p,p+2\) (a binary additive problem) the **minor arcs cannot be
  bounded** — there is no way to save a factor of \(\log\) on the minor arcs for a
  binary problem, and the major-arc main term is not provably dominant. (Same wall
  that leaves *binary* Goldbach open.)
- **Status as a route to TPC:** DEAD END unconditionally with current technology; it
  is the source of the *prediction*, not a proof.

### A9. Elliott–Halberstam / generalized EH as conditional inputs (1968→)
- **Proves (conditionally):** EH (level of distribution \(\vartheta = 1-\varepsilon\)
  for primes in APs) + GPY/Maynard gives small explicit gaps (\(\le 12\), \(\le 6\)
  under GEH). Bombieri–Vinogradov gives \(\vartheta = 1/2\) unconditionally; Zhang
  broke past \(1/2\) for smooth moduli.
- **Does NOT prove:** TPC even if EH/GEH were theorems — see A7's barrier theorem.
  EH is about *averaged* distribution of primes in APs; it does not break parity.
- **Status as a route to TPC:** DEAD END for the full conjecture even granting the
  conjectures; ACTIVE as conditional inputs for the bounded-gaps program.

### A10. Chowla / Liouville-correlation and "parity-breaking" conjectures
- **Status of the field:** The Liouville function \(\lambda(n)=(-1)^{\Omega(n)}\) is
  the quantitative carrier of parity. Sarnak/Chowla-type conjectures
  (\(\sum_{n\le x}\lambda(n)\lambda(n+2)=o(x)\)) and recent breakthroughs
  (Matomäki–Radziwiłł on averages of multiplicative functions in short intervals,
  2016; Tao's logarithmic Chowla for two-point correlations, 2015; Tao–Teräväinen)
  are the genuine *parity-breaking* results — the only known way to get information a
  pure sieve cannot.
- **Proves toward TPC:** nothing *directly* yet; but this is the only family of
  techniques that is not, in principle, parity-blocked.
- **Status as a route to TPC:** PROMISING in principle, ACTIVE as a research
  frontier — this is where "extra arithmetic input beyond the sieve" would have to
  come from. Distant from TPC, but not provably barred the way A1–A9 are.

### A11. Other distinct angles (recorded for completeness; ergodic / automorphic / algebraic)
- **Ergodic / dynamical (Furstenberg-style):** powerful for *additive* patterns in
  sets of positive density and for primes via Green–Tao (arithmetic progressions in
  primes). But Green–Tao produces *long APs*, not bounded gaps, and crucially needs a
  pseudorandomness/positive-relative-density input that does not pin a *fixed* gap of
  2. DEAD END for TPC.
- **Automorphic forms / spectral (Kloosterman, shifted convolution sums):** central
  to pushing distribution-of-primes estimates (e.g. beyond Bombieri–Vinogradov) and
  to A5/A7. They *strengthen sieve inputs* but do not break parity. ACTIVE as
  technical inputs, DEAD END as a standalone route to TPC.
- **Polynomial / function-field analogue:** TPC analogue is *proved* in
  \(\mathbb{F}_q[t]\) for large \(q\) (Sawin–Shusterman, 2018+) using geometric /
  monodromy methods with no parity obstruction in that setting. **Does NOT transfer**
  to \(\mathbb{Z}\): the geometric input (a large field of constants, equidistribution
  via Deligne) has no analogue over \(\mathbb{Z}\). DEAD END as a transfer, but a
  genuine signal that TPC is "true and provable where the parity obstruction is
  absent."

## The Apply: Selberg's parity principle in the twin-prime setup, stated to expose the wall

I now apply the parity principle directly to the twin-prime problem to derive *why*
A1–A9 stall at the same place, so the project is oriented around the right node.

**Setup.** To detect twins by a sieve, one studies the sifting set
\[
\mathcal{A} = \{\, n(n+2) : n \le x \,\}, \qquad
\mathcal{P} = \{\text{primes}\},
\]
and sieves \(\mathcal{A}\) of small prime factors below a level \(z=x^{1/s}\). A
prime pair \((n,n+2)\) corresponds to \(n(n+2)\) being a product of *exactly two*
primes both \(>z\) (when \(z\) is taken near \(\sqrt{x}\), i.e. \(s\to 2\)). Sieve
weights estimate \(S(\mathcal{A},\mathcal{P},z) = \sum_{n\le x}\prod_{p\le
z}\mathbf{1}[p\nmid n(n+2)]\) against the density function \(g\), here of dimension
\(\kappa = 2\) (two residue classes \(0,-2 \bmod p\) are removed for odd \(p\)).

**Parity principle (Selberg), specialized.** Sieve weights are sensitive only to the
function \(d \mapsto \mu(d)\)-type sign data through divisors \(d\) supported on
primes \(\le z\); they are *insensitive* to \(\lambda(n)=(-1)^{\Omega(n)}\) on the
unsifted large-prime part. Concretely, for the dimension-\(\kappa\) sieve there exist
two nonnegative "comparison" sequences \(a_n, b_n\) supported on \(n(n+2)\)-type
integers with:
- *identical* sieve data up to level \(z\) (same congruence sums for all \(d\le
  z^{?}\) within the level of distribution), yet
- \(\sum a_n \mathbf{1}[\Omega(n(n+2)) \text{ even}]\) and the odd-counterpart are
  swapped between \(a\) and \(b\).
A sieve cannot separate them, so its lower bound for "\(\Omega(n(n+2))=2\)" (twins,
two primes \(>z\)) cannot be made positive: the best provable lower bound is
\(\ge (\text{positive})\cdot[\text{count of }\Omega=2] - (\text{equal})\cdot
[\text{count of }\Omega=2\text{ or }4\ldots]\), and the parity-blind sieve charges
the \(\Omega \in \{2,4,\dots\}\) (and the odd cases for $P_2$) against you.

**Consequence (the wall, informally).** Any method whose only arithmetic input is
- (i) the *density/level-of-distribution* of \(\mathcal{A}\) in arithmetic
  progressions (Bombieri–Vinogradov, EH, GEH, Zhang-type smooth-moduli estimates),
  and
- (ii) *nonnegative combinatorial sieve weights* (Brun, Selberg \(\Lambda^2\),
  GPY, Maynard–Tao multidimensional weights),

is **invariant under the parity involution** and therefore cannot produce a positive
lower bound for the count of a *fixed* prime pair such as \((n,n+2)\). This is exactly
why A1–A9 reach bounded gaps / \(P_2\) / conditional gap \(\le 6\) but **provably not**
gap \(=2\) infinitely often. The only escape route is **external parity-breaking
input** (A10: Chowla/Liouville-type cancellation), which no current theorem supplies
at the strength TPC needs.

**Audit note (what is rigorous vs. heuristic in the above).** The *existence* of
parity-blind comparison sequences is a theorem in the precise forms due to Selberg
and, in modern sharp form, **Bombieri's "asymptotic sieve"** (1976) and the
**Friedlander–Iwaniec parity** results: the asymptotic sieve theorem states that
under a level-of-distribution hypothesis the sieve detects almost-primes but provably
*cannot* detect primes unless an additional bilinear/"Type II" hypothesis (a
parity-breaking input) is supplied. The Polymath8b barrier theorem is the precise
quantitative form for the multidimensional sieve. The "informal consequence" above is
the *correct* high-level reading of these theorems; the candidate lemma below is the
attempt to state it sharply enough to certify, and is exactly what I will hand to
DISTILL — I am **not** asserting it as established yet.

I did **not** introduce a candidate lemma to DISTILL *this* step (BEGIN is a survey +
