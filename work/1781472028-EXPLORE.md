## 2. The actual mechanism: WHERE the log weight is load-bearing

I open the proof structure (cited at the search-surfaced/quoted level; arXiv 403 here).
There are **three** distinct places the logarithmic weight `1/n` does work, and they are
not interchangeable:

### (M1) Dilation invariance of the measure `dn/n` (the telescoping the log buys).

The Liouville function is multiplicative at small primes: `λ(pn) = −λ(n)`. Tao's argument
plays the value of the correlation off against its **dilate by a prime `p`**. The key
algebraic fact is the reindexing identity (m = pn):

```
   Σ_{n≤x} f(pn)/(pn)  =  (1/p) Σ_{m≤px, p|m} f(m)/m .
```

Because the **logarithmic measure `dn/n` is scale-invariant** (`d(pn)/(pn) = dn/n`),
dilating `n ↦ pn` is *nearly measure-preserving*: it costs only an **`O(log p / log x)`
boundary term**. This is the "telescoping" the log-average buys. The **ordinary measure
`dn` is NOT scale-invariant** — dilation by `p` rescales total mass by a factor `p` — so
the same play, applied to a Cesàro average, does **not** close: the dilate lives at a
different scale with `p`-times the mass and there is no cheap identity returning it to the
original average.

This is the precise sense of the literature's statement that "certain equations would not
hold for measures corresponding to arbitrary invariant means" — the equations are the
dilation identities, and `dn/n` is the *unique* (up to scale) dilation-invariant mean on
`ℕ` that makes them hold with an `O(log p/log x)` error.

**I audited (M1) numerically (Part B below): it is confirmed cleanly.**

### (M2) The entropy decrement: a scale that cannot be named in advance.

The dilation play does not work at a *single* fixed prime `p`; one needs it simultaneously
over a *range* of primes / scales, and the argument must **pigeonhole to find a good scale
`P`** at which a certain mutual-information / entropy quantity stops decreasing. That scale
`P` is **not specifiable in advance** — it is extracted at runtime from a
monotone-bounded entropy sequence.

This is the load-bearing coupling to the log weight: a logarithmic average is **stable
under the dilations `n ↦ pn`** for `p` in the found range (by M1), so a *runtime-found*
scale is harmless. An ordinary average is **not** dilation-stable, so a runtime-found
scale `P` (which can be a positive power of `x`) **changes the average by a non-negligible
amount**, and the conclusion at the found scale does not transfer back to the average you
wanted to bound. (Search-surfaced statement, Tao's exposition: "the entropy decrement
argument requires picking a scale that cannot be specified in advance, which is not a
problem for logarithmic averages — quite stable under dilations — but is problematic for
ordinary averages.")

### (M3) The MR(T) short-interval / circle-method input.

Matomäki–Radziwiłł short-interval control of multiplicative functions + the
Hardy–Littlewood circle method handle the "major-arc"/structured part once the entropy
step has supplied the weak-independence across scales. This input is *not* itself the
obstruction to de-averaging — it survives at almost all scales (see §3) — so it is **not**
where (b2) breaks. (Important for localization: it rules out "improve MRT" as the residual.)

**Net of §2:** the log weight is load-bearing through **(M1)+(M2)** — the *dilation
invariance* that makes a *runtime-found, unspecifiable scale* harmless. (M3) is not the
blocker. So the residual of (b2) lives in the **scale-selection / dilation** layer, not in
the multiplicative-function-in-short-intervals layer.

## 3. APPLY the almost-all-scales structure theorem: where de-averaging SUCCEEDS

Here is the decisive literature fact the prior graph had not localized. Tao–Teräväinen,
**"The structure of correlations of multiplicative functions at almost all scales"**
(arXiv:1809.02518; Algebra & Number Theory 13 (9) (2019) 2103–2150) proves
(search-surfaced/quoted level):

> Most recent results on the logarithmically averaged Chowla/Elliott conjectures can be
> **extended to their non-logarithmically averaged counterparts, so long as one excludes a
> set of exceptional scales of logarithmic density zero.** At almost all scales (outside a
> set of logarithmic density zero) the non-logarithmic averages behave like their
> logarithmic counterparts **except for a possible additional twisting by an Archimedean
> character** `n ↦ n^{it}`. In particular the `k`-point Chowla conjecture holds for `k`
> odd or `k = 2` **for all scales `X` outside a set of zero logarithmic density.**

Applying this to (b2) yields a clean **success/failure split** that replaces the
monolithic "wall":

**De-averaging SUCCEEDS, unconditionally, at almost all scales.** The two-point Chowla
`Σ_{X<n≤2X} λ(n)λ(n+2) = o(X)` is a **theorem for all `X` outside a set of logarithmic
density zero.** So the entropy/dilation machinery does *not* fail generically — it fails
only on a thin (log-density-zero) exceptional set of scales, and even there only up to an
**Archimedean character twist `n^{it}`**.

This is a genuine reduction of the *statement* of the obstruction (not of its difficulty):
(b2) is **not** "prove de-averaging from scratch"; it is exactly the two residual gaps
named in §4.

## 4. Where de-averaging FAILS: the residual, as a single crisp statement

The almost-all-scales theorem isolates **two** residual obstructions, and *both* must be
removed to get even Cesàro two-point Chowla (and a *third*, uniformity, to reach C5):

**(R1) The exceptional set of scales (log-density zero → density / everywhere).**
The theorem permits a set `E ⊂ {scales}` of **logarithmic density zero** on which the
non-log average may misbehave. A log-density-zero set can still be **infinite and even of
positive natural (upper) density** — e.g. the dyadic ranges `[X, 2X]` with `X ∈ [2^{2^k},
2^{2^k+k}]` are log-density zero but Cesàro-visible. To get the **Cesàro** statement at
*every* large scale one must **eliminate `E` entirely** (upgrade "almost all scales" to
"all scales"). This is exactly the part the entropy decrement *cannot* deliver: the entropy
argument is what *creates* the unspecified good scale, and dualizing it to "good at *every*
scale" is precisely the **random-graph expansion** that is open (the entropy step finesses
the absence of expansion by only ever needing *one* good scale, found at runtime).

**(R2) The Archimedean character twist `n^{it}`.** Outside `E`, the non-log average
matches the log one **up to a twist by `n^{it}`** for some real `t = t(X)` possibly growing
with `X`. Killing this twist is the **Fourier-uniformity** problem (see §5). The log
average is insensitive to the twist (the `1/n` weight against `n^{it}` is the `ζ`-type
integral that self-averages); the Cesàro average is **not** — a genuine `n^{it}` oscillation
in `λ` at scale `X` survives a non-log average and is exactly an unresolved possibility.

**(R3, only for C5) Uniformity in `d`.** Even granting Cesàro two-point Chowla at every
scale, C5 demands the bound `|Σ_{n≤x, n≡a(d)} λ(n)λ(n+2)| ≤ δ(x)·x/d` **uniformly over `d ≤
x^{1−ε}` and all `a`**. The almost-all-scales theorem is a *global* (no AP, `d=1`)
statement; the AP refinement re-imports the `L∞`-over-residues / edge difficulty already
recorded as SO-L² / L-QUB-reform. So (R3) sits *on top of* (R1)+(R2).

**Crisp residual statement (the sharpened sub-obstruction, call it `SO-deavg`):**

> **`SO-deavg` (OPEN).** Cesàro two-point Chowla `Σ_{n≤x} λ(n)λ(n+2)=o(x)` is equivalent,
> given Tao–Teräväinen 2019, to **(R1)** removing the log-density-zero exceptional set of
> scales **and (R2)** ruling out the Archimedean twist `n^{it}` at those scales — i.e. to
> **local Fourier uniformity of `λ` at every scale** (not just almost every). C5 demands,
> in addition, **(R3)** the uniform-in-`d` AP refinement. The single mechanism behind both
> (R1) and (R2) is that the entropy/dilation method produces a good scale that **cannot be
> specified in advance**, harmless under the dilation-invariant `dn/n` but not under `dn`;
> dualizing "one runtime good scale" to "every scale" is the **open random-graph expansion**.

This is strictly sharper than the prior "remove the averaging = open random-graph
expansion": it says *what* is left (R1 exceptional scales + R2 the `n^{it}` twist, = local
Fourier uniformity at every scale), *why* each is left (dilation non-invariance of `dn`;
the unspecifiable runtime scale), and *what is NOT the blocker* (M3 / MRT short-interval
input, which already survives at almost all scales).

## 5. The bridge to Fourier uniformity (why (R2)/(R1) = local Fourier uniformity)

The **local Fourier-uniformity conjecture** (Tao 2015 blog "A conjectural local
Fourier-uniformity of the Liouville function"; Matomäki–Radziwiłł–Tao, arXiv:1812.01224,
Inventiones; higher-order Tao–Teräväinen arXiv:2007.15644) is:

> For every `H = H(X) → ∞` (arbitrarily slowly),
> `Σ_{X<x≤2X} sup_{α∈ℝ} |Σ_{x<n≤x+H} λ(n) e(nα)| = o(HX)` as `X → ∞`.

Cited facts (search-surfaced):
- The **local Fourier-uniformity conjecture implies the (log-averaged) Chowla and Sarnak
  conjectures** for `λ`. (Tao 2015 blog; standard.)
- MR T (arXiv:1812.01224) prove the **`s=1` (linear-phase `e(nα)`) case on average** for
  `H = X^θ`, any fixed `θ > 0` (improving the previous `θ > 5/8`). The remaining gap to the
  *full* conjecture is exactly **(a)** shrinking `H` from `X^θ` to arbitrarily slowly
  growing, and **(b)** removing the on-average over `X` (i.e. handling *every* scale, not
  almost every).

Putting §3 and §5 together: the **`n^{it}` twist of (R2) is a linear phase** (it is the
`α = t·(...)` / Archimedean-character direction in the `sup_α` of Fourier uniformity), and
the **exceptional-scales set of (R1) is exactly the "on-average over `X`" slack** in the MRT
theorem. So:

> **`SO-deavg` ⟺ (full, every-scale) local Fourier uniformity of `λ`** (degree 1 for the
> two-point pattern), the **`H ≍ X` / every-scale endpoint** of the MRT line — plus (R3)
> the AP-uniformity on top for C5.

This is the same wall as C5 (confirming the campaign verdict — de-averaging does not escape
C5), but it is now pinned to a **named, actively-studied open conjecture** with a precise
boundary (MRT did `θ>0` on average; the open part is *every scale* + *arbitrarily short
`H`*), rather than to the diffuse "random-graph expansion." That is the headway.

**A second known obstruction confirms this is not merely unattempted but structurally
hard:** Frantzikinakis–Host (arXiv:1708.00677 and the dynamical-model construction)
exhibit a **dynamical system modelling `λ` that is an explicit obstruction to solving the
Fourier-uniformity conjecture purely by dynamical/ergodic methods.** So the ergodic route
to (R2) has a *known internal obstruction*; one cannot close `SO-deavg` by the
Frantzikinakis–Host structure theorem alone. (This rules out "just use the ergodic
structure theorem" as a route — another non-blocker eliminated.)
