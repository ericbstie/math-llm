## ATTACK K — Missed-sibling hunt #1: pair correlation / spectral (Montgomery, Bogomolny–Keating, Keating–Smith)

**The attack.** If TPC can be reached through the statistics of zeros of ζ, the missing
input might be a statement about **zeros** (pair correlation / GUE) that is a *distinct*
open object from C5, not a λ-correlation. If so, C5 is not the unique crux.

**What I found (primary-source snippets, Berry–Keating–Smith line; Goldston–Montgomery;
Montgomery–Soundararajan; Conrey–Snaith ratios):**

- The relationship between the Hardy–Littlewood prime-pair conjecture and the **two-point
  correlation of Riemann zeros** is an **EQUIVALENCE**, not a one-way reduction:
  *"the Hardy–Littlewood twin prime conjecture is equivalent to an asymptotic formula for
  the two-point correlation function of Riemann zeros... it was known that the
  Hardy–Littlewood conjecture implies the pair correlation formula, and the reverse
  implication also holds"* (Keating–Smith, "Twin prime correlations from the pair
  correlation of Riemann zeros," arXiv:1903.07057; "Pair correlation and twin primes
  revisited," Proc. R. Soc. A 472 (2016), arXiv:1604.06124).
- That equivalence is established **"through a formal/heuristic Fourier inversion
  calculation"** — i.e. it is **not a rigorous reduction**.
- The pair-correlation formula itself is **conjectural**: *"these remain conditional and
  conjectural results rather than fully proven theorems."* Montgomery's actual *theorem*
  is only a **restricted-support, RH-conditional** statement.
- Goldston–Montgomery: pair correlation ⇔ an asymptotic for the **variance of primes in
  short intervals**; Montgomery–Soundararajan: that variance follows from HL **under
  assumptions**. All conditional/conjectural.

**Verdict: NOT a distinct sibling — and strictly WEAKER as an escape than C5.** The
spectral object is *equivalent in strength* to (a smooth form of) the full
Hardy–Littlewood prime-pair conjecture, which is **stronger** than C5 (HL is an
asymptotic *with the singular-series constant*; C5 is only `o(x/d)` cancellation of a
λ-correlation). The equivalence is heuristic, and the zero-side statement is itself
unproven (only RH-conditional restricted pair correlation is a theorem). So the spectral
route (i) provides **no theorem at TPC strength**, and (ii) its missing input is *not a
new, easier open object* — it is a *harder* object (full HL) that *implies* C5.
Classification: like A8, **not an escape**; missing input stronger than C5. Does **not**
break the terminal claim. (If anything it corroborates it: every honest route bottoms out
at a prime-pair/λ-correlation statement.)

## ATTACK L — Missed-sibling hunt #2: Siegel-zero / Heath-Brown route (the most serious candidate)

**The attack.** Heath-Brown (1983) proved a *conditional* twin-prime theorem whose
hypothesis is **the existence of Siegel zeros** — a *different* open object than a
λ-correlation bound. Tao–Teräväinen (2021) and the IMRN 2023 paper extend it. If
"Siegel zeros exist" is a genuine sibling OPEN node, C5 is not unique.

**What I found (primary-source snippets):**

- **Heath-Brown, "Prime Twins and Siegel Zeros," Proc. London Math. Soc. (3) 47 (1983)
  193–224.** The theorem is a **DICHOTOMY** (Tao's exposition, "Heath-Brown's theorem on
  prime twins and Siegel zeroes," 2015-08-26): *"at least one of two statements must be
  true: either there are infinitely many twin primes, OR there exists a constant such
  that for every real Dirichlet character the associated L-function has no zeroes in a
  certain interval [i.e. no Siegel zeros]."* Equivalently: *(infinitely many Siegel
  zeros) ⟹ (infinitely many twin primes, with the HL asymptotic at infinitely many
  scales).*
- **The hypothesis is believed FALSE.** GRH precludes Siegel zeros; *"the hypothesis of
  infinitely many Siegel zeros is believed to be false"* / *"considered highly unlikely."*
  Tao: this is an *"illusory universe... notoriously impossible to eliminate."*
- **Mechanism = a FAILURE of Möbius/Liouville pseudorandomness.** Tao: *"in an alternate
  universe in which the Möbius function is strongly correlated with some structured
  functions and Dirichlet characters, leading to the existence of Siegel zeroes, the
  parity problem obstruction disappears."*
- Tao–Teräväinen 2021 (JLMS 2022) and "Siegel zeros, twin primes, Goldbach..." (IMRN
  2023, arXiv:2112.11412) **improve the quantitative range** and generalize to tuples,
  but remain **conditional on the same (believed-false) Siegel-zero hypothesis.**

**Verdict: NOT a distinct sibling OPEN node, and NOT an unconditional escape — for two
independent reasons, both decisive.**

1. **It is the wrong polarity of the same object.** A Siegel zero is *precisely* the
   statement that λ (equivalently μ) **correlates** with a real Dirichlet character — the
   exact *negation* of the cancellation asserted by C5. So "Siegel zeros exist" is not an
   *independent* arithmetic input one could supply alongside C5; it is the **anti-thesis
   of C5's content**, the same λ-distribution-in-APs phenomenon viewed from the
   correlation side. Resolving it is not a separate task disjoint from C5 — it lives on
   the *same* axis (distribution of λ over arithmetic progressions). The literature makes
   this explicit: the Siegel zero is what makes "the parity problem obstruction
   disappear," and the parity obstruction is exactly C5's heuristic content.

2. **It cannot be used as an unconditional route.** To extract TPC from Heath-Brown you
   must *prove infinitely many Siegel zeros exist* — a statement believed false and at
   least as hard as refuting a piece of GRH. A conditional theorem "FALSE-hypothesis ⟹
   TPC" is not a path to an unconditional proof. (It is the dichotomy's *other* horn —
   TPC — that everyone expects to be the true one, precisely because Siegel zeros are
   expected not to exist.) So this is structurally like A11c/A10: a genuine *conditional*
   result, not an unconditional escape, whose unconditional content reduces to a
   λ-in-APs statement = C5's axis.

**Finding for EXPLORE (the one refinement of this pass).** The current graph buries this
route inside C5 sub-fact (iii) as "Tao–Teräväinen almost-all/Siegel-conditional only,"
**without naming Heath-Brown 1983 or stating why the Siegel-zero hypothesis is not a
distinct sibling.** Given it is the single most plausible candidate for a "different open
object," the graph is **expositorily incomplete** until it explicitly records the
Heath-Brown dichotomy and the "wrong-polarity-of-C5 + believed-false-hypothesis"
argument. This does **not** reopen terminal state (it is not a sibling), but EXPLORE
should add a node/scope-note (proposed below as **C6**, ESTABLISHED-IN-LITERATURE) so the
declaration's "no missed angle" claim is *demonstrated*, not asserted. A "no missed angle"
declaration that does not address the most famous conditional twin-prime theorem is
under-audited.

  λ-correlation content as C5. **This STRENGTHENS the terminal claim**: binary Goldbach
  and TPC share *one* crux, not two distinct ones (directly answering the prompt's "does

  sets and the Gowers-norm input is exactly the *Type-I-side* structure the parity barrier

- **(c) Automorphic / GL(2) shifted convolution: inside the input-side of C1, no sibling.**

**Verdict: no distinct sibling.** (a) reduces to the *same* object as C5 (and strengthens
uniqueness); (b) is a dead end for fixed gaps; (c) is inside C1's input side. Terminal
claim survives.
