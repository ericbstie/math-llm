## PART 2 — Answering the four questions, with citations

### Q1. The class — what axioms define it; what is [7]?

Sawin uses **two** axiom systems, related by Remark 3.5:

- **His own class: Definition 1.1 (p. 2).** A *dynamical model for λ* (λ : N → ±1 multiplicative) is a
  tuple `(X, µ, F, T, M, In)` — probability space `(X,µ)`, `F : X → ±1`, measure-preserving
  `T : X → X`, measure-preserving `M : X → Ẑ`, and for each `n` a map `In : M⁻¹(nẐ) → X` — satisfying,
  a.e.:
  1. `M(x) ∈ nẐ ⟹ n·M(In(x)) = M(x)`;
  2. `M(T(x)) = M(x) + 1`;
  3. `M(x) ∈ nẐ ⟹ T(In(x)) = In(Tⁿ(x))`;
  4. `M(x) ∈ nmẐ ⟹ Im(In(x)) = Inm(x)`;
  5. `M(x) ∈ nẐ ⟹ F(In(x)) = λ(n)·F(x)`;
  plus pushforward `(In)_* µ = µ/n` on `M⁻¹(nZ)`. (Intuition, Remark 1.2: `X` = the naturals, `µ` =
  uniform measure on a long interval, `F` = λ, `T` = increment, `M` = `N → Ẑ`, `In` = division-by-`n`.)

- **Tao's class: [7, Proposition 1], axioms (i)–(iv).** Sawin does not reprint the four axioms
  verbatim, but pins down the **first** one exactly (p. 2): axiom **(i)** = *"the dynamical system
  actually arises as a limit of Liouville."* By **Remark 3.5 (p. 11)**, after an inverse-limit
  construction `(X', µ', F, M)` + the affine action `φ(n) = an + b`, a dynamical model satisfies Tao's
  **(ii), (iii), (iv)** and "may or may not satisfy axiom **(i)**."

- **Identification of [7]** (reference list, p. 12-13): **[7] = Terence Tao, "Furstenberg limits of

### Q2. What is dropped — and is the anomalous model's membership contingent on it?

The **first axiom of [7, Proposition 1]** — verbatim, p. 2 — is *"the dynamical system actually arises
as a limit of Liouville."* Sawin's method is to **replace axiom (i) by a weaker one** "which
encapsulates what has been proven on the analytic side (or a potential future analytic result)" — and
his concrete weaker replacement is **`d`-Fourier uniformity** (Definition 1.4, p. 2-3): the
Gowers–Host–Kra `U^d` seminorm of `F` on `(X, µ, F, T)` vanishes; equivalently `λ` (resp. `F`) does
not correlate with any `(d−1)`-step nilsystem.

His anomalous models (Theorem 3.1, p. 7-8; Theorem 3.2, p. 9-10) are built as
**`X = (R^{d+1}/H) × Ẑ`** (Theorem 3.1) and `X = (R^{d+1}/H) × (Z/q)^× × Ẑ` (Theorem 3.2), where
`H` is the lattice of degree-`d` polynomials taking even-integer values on integers — i.e. **`d`-step
nilsystems (polynomial-phase / torus systems)**. p. 6 (intro): *"Our dynamical systems will be created
from polynomials of degree d. (This is unsurprising, as they are the simplest example of a dynamical
system which is `d`-Fourier uniform but not `d+1`-Fourier uniform.)"*

**Membership IS contingent on dropping axiom (i).** These polynomial-phase systems are **not** genuine
Furstenberg limits of Liouville; they are *general nilsystems* engineered to satisfy the weaker
analytic axiom (`U^d`-uniformity, + the algebraic identities (1)-(5) with a formal `F(In x)=λ(n)F(x)`)
while exhibiting **anomalous sign-pattern behaviour** (Theorem 3.2: a `d`-Fourier-uniform model in
which a prescribed long sign pattern *does not appear*; Remark 3.4: a model that satisfies 2-point and
odd-point Chowla but **fails** `(d+2)`-point Chowla). They obstruct progress **precisely because** they
satisfy everything *except* axiom (i): p. 2, *"we can test the boundaries of these methods by replacing
that axiom by a weaker one."*

### Q3. Scope of "methods that apply to the whole class" — does it include the strong-stationarity / structure-theorem input?

Sawin is explicit about which methods are class-invariant (Abstract; §1, p. 1-2):
*"Most recent progress on the Chowla conjectures and sign patterns ... uses methods that apply to any
dynamical system in this class."* These are:
- **[3]** MRT short-interval (used only to give vanishing `U^1`: p. 2-3, "The result of Matomäki and
  Radziwiłł implies that any Furstenburg limit of λ has vanishing GHK `U^1` norm");
- **[4]** MRT–Tao sign-pattern combinatorics;
- **[5]/[8]** Tao and Tao–Teräväinen information-theoretic / **entropy-decrement** methods for the
  log-averaged Chowla conjecture (two-point and odd-order).
His **own** "positive results" §4 (Propositions 4.1, 4.2, p. 11-12) assume **only `Equation 1`**
(`E_x ∏_{i} F(T^{c_i} x) = 0`, the *dynamical Chowla conjecture*) — nothing finer.

Critically: **all of these apply because they DO NOT use axiom (i)** (§1, p. 2: Tao's [5] "can be
deduced from [3] purely within the setting of limits of Liouville ... it does not use the first
axiom ... The same is true for [4]"). They use multiplicativity-at-small/large-primes, short-interval
cancellation, entropy decrement, and Fourier-uniformity — the structure Sawin's models **retain**.

**Does this scope include the strong-stationarity / "no irrational spectrum" structure-theorem input?**
**NO — that input is of a strictly finer type, and it is exactly the content of the DROPPED axiom (i).**
Evidence, decisive:

- **The FH structure theorem is [1]; the strong-stationarity / ergodic-weight machinery is [2].** Sawin
  cites both but does **not** put either among the "methods that apply to the whole class." The
  whole-class methods are [3],[4],[5],[8] (short-interval/combinatorial/entropy), not the FH
  spectral/strong-stationarity structure theory.

- **Sawin's `d=1` anomalous model IS FH's Liouville counterexample.** p. 9 (verbatim, audited):
  > *"In the case d = 1, the dynamical system we construct here matches the dynamical system
  > `(T², m_{T²}, T)` defined in [2, p. 7] and also studied there as a counterexample for the Liouville
  > function."*
  `(T², m_{T²}, T)` is a **rotation on the 2-torus = a Kronecker system**: its spectrum is **pure point
  with irrational eigenvalues**. So Sawin's `d=1` anomalous model carries **exactly the irrational
  (`n^{it}`-type) Kronecker eigenvalue** that the FH/Lemańczyk "no irrational spectrum / strong
  stationarity" structure theorem would have to kill. It does **NOT** share the "no irrational
  spectrum" invariant — *that is the whole point of it being a counterexample.*

### Q4. The verdict on `ERG-2`

**The literal sub-claim — "Sawin's anomalous models share the FH structure-theorem invariants
*exactly*" — is FALSE/imprecise as worded: the `d=1` anomalous model is a Kronecker rotation `(T²,T)`
with irrational pure-point spectrum (it is FH's own counterexample [2, p. 7]), so it does NOT share the
FH "no-irrational-spectrum / strong-stationarity" invariant.**

**BUT this is NOT a handle on (R2). The wall is STRENGTHENED.** Here is the careful reasoning (the part
that matters, and where I refuse to over-celebrate):

The "handle" hypothesis would be: *because Sawin's anomalous models do NOT share the FH
no-irrational-spectrum invariant, the FH/Lemańczyk strong-stationarity structure theorem ESCAPES
Sawin's counterexample, hence applying it to the genuine Furstenberg system of λ could force
`t(X)=0`.* Sawin **anticipates and closes exactly this**, and the closure is *why* there is no handle:

1. The structure theorem *does* escape Sawin's polynomial-phase counterexamples — **because those
   counterexamples drop axiom (i)** ("arises as a limit of Liouville"). The escape is bought entirely
   by the dropped axiom.
2. But axiom (i) is precisely *"the system actually arises as a limit of Liouville."* The **genuine**
   Furstenberg system of λ satisfies axiom (i) by construction. So to use the strong-stationarity
   structure theorem to force `t(X)=0` for the *real* λ, you must invoke **exactly the property
   (axiom (i)) that Sawin's models lack.**
3. The content of axiom (i) that does the work is **strong stationarity** (the dilation invariance
   `n ↦ pn`, equivalently the multiplicativity-respecting structure of a true Liouville limit). And
   **strong stationarity is a LOGARITHMIC phenomenon**: FH–Lemańczyk (arXiv:2304.03121) prove it holds
   for the **logarithmic** Furstenberg system but **PROVABLY FAILS for Cesàro / per-scale** systems.
   This is the corrected Leak-1 already in the sheet — and Sawin's paper *confirms its shape*: the
   whole-class progress techniques (which **don't** use axiom (i)) are exactly the **log-level** /
   short-interval / entropy methods; the **finer** input (axiom (i) / strong stationarity) is what
   separates the genuine λ-limit from a nilsystem.

So the net is fully consistent and now **pinned to primary text**:
- The **progress-technique** half of ERG-2's *conclusion* — "no class-invariant progress technique
  (MRT/entropy/Fourier-uniformity) forces `t(X)=0`" — is **TRUE and now verified** (Abstract; §1;
  Def 1.4; Props 4.1/4.2 with only Equation 1).
- The **structure-theorem** half — does the FH strong-stationarity input share the anomalous models'
  invariants? — is resolved: the input is **NOT class-invariant**; it requires axiom (i), which the
  anomalous models drop. **But the part of axiom (i) you would need (strong stationarity) lives only
  at the logarithmic level (its Cesàro analogue provably fails, 2304.03121), so it gives NOTHING at
  the Cesàro / per-scale level that (R2) demands.** Escaping Sawin at the **log** level is already
  *known* — that is exactly why the log-Chowla two-point theorem [5] is a theorem. (R2) needs Cesàro,

**Therefore: `ERG-2` resolves as FALSE-AS-WORDED, NO HANDLE — wall strengthened.** The previously-OPEN
