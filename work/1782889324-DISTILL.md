## Part 3 — Attack on L-TRACE-REFORM

### 3.1 [TRACE-SUFF] (⟸): confirm it is exactly Lidskii — rigorous

The ⟸ direction is exactly **Lidskii's theorem** in its inequality form: for trace-class `A` on a
separable Hilbert space, `trace(A)=Σ_k λ_k(A)` (sum of eigenvalues, with multiplicity), whence
`|trace(A)| ≤ Σ_k |λ_k(A)| ≤ Σ_k σ_k(A) = ‖A‖₁` (the middle step is Weyl's majorant inequality
`Σ|λ_k| ≤ Σσ_k`). So if `trace(A_x)=C5(x)` and `‖A_x‖₁=O(x^{1/2+ε})`, then
`|C5(x)| ≤ ‖A_x‖₁ = O(x^{1/2+ε})`. Reference: B. Simon, *Trace Ideals and Their Applications*, 2nd ed.,
AMS Math. Surveys & Monographs 120 (2005), Ch. 3 (Lidskii's theorem) and the Weyl majorant inequality
therein. Numerical confirmation on non-normal matrices (`|trace| ≤ ‖·‖₁`, and `trace = Σ eigenvalues`):

```python
import numpy as np
rng=np.random.default_rng(0); worst=1e9
for _ in range(2000):
    d=rng.integers(2,12)
    A=rng.standard_normal((d,d))+1j*rng.standard_normal((d,d))
    ev=np.linalg.eigvals(A); sv=np.linalg.svd(A,compute_uv=False)
    assert abs(abs(np.sum(ev))-abs(np.trace(A)))<1e-8        # trace = sum of eigenvalues (Lidskii)
    worst=min(worst, sv.sum()-abs(np.sum(ev)))               # ||A||_1 - |trace|
print("min(||A||_1 - |trace|) over 2000 matrices = %.4f  (>=0 confirms |trace|<=||.||_1)"%worst)
```
```
min(||A||_1 - |trace|) over 2000 matrices = 0.0323  (>=0 confirms |trace|<=||.||_1)
```
**[TRACE-SUFF] is RIGOROUS** — a textbook sufficient condition. It is, however, **not a bound on C5** by
itself: it is conditional on the existence of the `A_x` with a small trace norm, which is unproven.

**⟹ verdict (precise, as demanded).** Enforcing the structural clause, ⟹ is **(ii) UNPROVEN**, not (i)
