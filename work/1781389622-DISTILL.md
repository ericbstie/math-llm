### Attack P2 — CONCRETELY FALSIFY the purity-only version (the most dangerous overclaim)

**Claim tested (the naive sub-claim).** "(1)+(2) ⟹ C5" — i.e. *purity / RH-strength alone* yields the
uniform two-point bound. This is the version that *feels* like it should work, so I attack it hardest and
demand a concrete object, not generic doubt.

**Attack, part (a) — the strength mismatch is real (Chowla-strength vs RH-strength).**
The unaveraged two-point λ-correlation at shift 2 is **Chowla-strength**. The hierarchy:
- **RH ⇏ the unaveraged correlation.** What is unconditionally known toward Chowla(2) is the
  *logarithmically averaged* two-point case (Tao, Forum Math. Pi 2016, arXiv:1509.05422; quantitative
  improvements Pilatte arXiv:2310.19357). The **unaveraged fixed-shift** correlation ∑_{n≤x}λ(n)λ(n+2)=o(x)
  is **open**, and is **not known to follow even from GRH** — under GRH one still only accesses short-interval
  / sign-pattern / log-averaged consequences, not the unaveraged fixed-shift sum, let alone the
  uniform-in-d statement C5. (I deliberately do *not* lean on the converse "Chowla ⟹ RH": a careful read
  shows that implication is itself subtle — the naive short-interval analogue is *not* known to give full
  RH, only weaker variants. I drop it; the load-bearing direction for the attack is the established
  **RH/GRH ⇏ unaveraged Chowla(2)**, which is exactly the gap.)
  So the *output* of clause (2) (a purity/weight bound = the ℤ-analogue of RH over finite fields) is
  **strictly weaker** than the *target* C5. Purity-only cannot bridge that gap by logic alone.

**Attack, part (b) — the decisive concrete object: in the function field, purity IS a theorem and STILL
does not give twin primes.** This is the cleanest possible falsifier, because it removes every "but maybe
over ℤ it's different" hedge: take the world where clause (2) is **fully proven**.

- Over 𝔽_q[t], Deligne's purity (Weil II) is **Weil's theorem** — square-root cancellation for the
  relevant trace functions holds *unconditionally*. If "(1)+(2) ⟹ twin primes" were valid as a logical
  pattern, **Sawin–Shusterman would be a one-line corollary of Weil II.** It is not.
- Instead, Sawin–Shusterman (Annals 196 (2022), arXiv:1808.04001) require a **separate, hard geometric
  input on top of purity**: a *big-monodromy / equidistribution* statement. Confirmed this pass from the
  abstract/summary and Quanta's account: they "improve on the function field version of the **Burgess
  bound**," show "the **Möbius function over 𝔽_q[T] can be mimicked by Dirichlet characters** … when
  restricted to certain special subspaces," and feed a "**function field variant of a result by
  Fouvry–Michel** on exponential sums involving the Möbius function" to push the **level of distribution
  beyond 1/2**, whence twin primes. The Burgess-improvement / character-mimicry is precisely the
  **monodromy/equidistribution** content (clause (3)), and it is **not** delivered by purity (clause (2)).
- Structure of why purity is insufficient there: purity bounds each Frobenius eigenvalue by q^{(w+i)/2},
  giving √-cancellation **only if** the cohomology does not concentrate in the top degree and the
  monodromy **co/invariants vanish**. The vanishing of the top-weight (invariant) part is a *separate*
  statement, established by **computing the geometric monodromy group and proving it is big** (Katz's
  method) — exactly Sawin–Shusterman's extra labor. Purity is necessary, not sufficient.

### Attack P3 — FABRICATION AUDIT of clauses (1) and (3)

**Claim tested.** "Is any step asserted as if proven when it is not?" — focus on clause (3) "big monodromy
over ℤ kills the top-weight invariants" and on whether clause (1) smuggles in the absent base field.

**Attack on clause (1).** Writing ∑_{n≤x}λ(n)λ(n+2) = ∑_i(−1)^i Tr(F^? | ℍ^i_c(𝒮, 𝓛⊗sh_2^*𝓛)) presupposes
(i) a base over which there is an **absolute Frobenius endomorphism F** whose iterates count points at
"scale x," and (ii) a constructible 𝓛 whose trace-of-Frobenius function is λ on ℤ. Over 𝔽_q[t] both exist
(F = q-power Frobenius; λ is a genuine trace function). **Over Spec ℤ neither is known to exist.** The
Connes–Consani arithmetic site carries a Frobenius-*like* ℝ_{>0}^× action, but the **trace formula
computing arithmetic sums is precisely the unproven Lefschetz-formula goal** (Attack P1). So clause (1),
read as an available equation over ℤ, **does smuggle in the absent base field**: the "= Tr(F^?|ℍ^•)" sign
is an assertion of the open formalism, not a manipulation. As an audited input it is **OPEN**. (As a
*conditional* statement "if such (H^•,F,𝓛) exist then the trace identity holds by Grothendieck–Lefschetz"
it is fine — but then its antecedent is the open C7 below.)

**Attack on clause (3) — the central fabrication test.** Transport clause (3) honestly and ask *what
statement over ℤ it actually is*. In the function-field proof, "big monodromy ⇒ top-weight invariants
vanish" is the geometric mechanism that **forces the main term of the correlation to cancel** — it is the
exact step that turns "there is a cohomology" into "the sum is small." Concretely (Katz / Weil II): if
G_geom acting on the sheaf 𝓕 = 𝓛⊗sh_a^*𝓛 has **no nonzero invariants/coinvariants** in the top degree,
then H^{top}_c(𝓕) = 0 and the alternating trace is dominated by strictly lower weight, i.e. the
correlation is o(main). The *content* of clause (3) is therefore identical to the assertion **"the shift-2
λ-correlation has no main term"** — which **is C5 itself** (the o(x/d) cancellation), now phrased as
vanishing of an invariant subspace.

So over ℤ, "prove big monodromy for the Liouville sheaf at shift 2" is **equivalent to / at least as hard
as C5**: it is the cohomological avatar of the Chowla cancellation. A few consequences, stated as the
attack's finding:
- Clause (3) does **not** reduce C5 to something *easier* in the monodromy direction — **it re-expresses
  C5** in sheaf-theoretic clothing ("a **cohomological C5**").
- Any step that *asserts* clause (3) over ℤ — "the monodromy is big, so the invariants vanish" — without a
  proof is therefore **asserting C5 as if proven**, i.e. **fabrication**. There is **no ℤ-object whose
  monodromy is known to be large** in the required sense. "This is how it works over 𝔽_q[t]" is **not an
  audit for the ℤ statement** (over 𝔽_q[t] the monodromy computation is the hard theorem, not a free
  input; transporting its *conclusion* without its proof is precisely the illegal move).

**What it found.** Clause (1) over ℤ is **OPEN** (it presupposes the absent Frobenius/trace formalism =
C7). Clause (3) over ℤ is **OPEN and is C5-in-disguise** (cohomological C5): proving it is equivalent to,
or harder than, C5. Neither may stand as audited; each is marked OPEN. No fabricated citation introduced.

### Attack P4 — the relocated node: NEW sibling, C5-in-disguise, or a third input (∞)?

**Claim tested.** "After transport, what exactly is the obstruction — a genuinely new open object, C5
re-expressed, or something else?"

**Attack.** Decompose what L-COH's hypothesis splits C5's content into, and classify each piece by whether

**Finding — it is BOTH, plus a third place:**

1. **A genuinely NEW, strictly BROADER open object (call it C7).** The existence over Spec ℤ (or 𝔽₁ / the
   Connes–Consani arithmetic site) of a **Weil-type cohomology with six functors, Poincaré–Verdier
   duality, an absolute Frobenius, and a Grothendieck–Lefschetz trace formula** realizing the
   counting/zeta equality is **not** C5: it is the **Deninger / Connes–Consani conjecture**, a recognized
   major open problem whose payoff (if it existed with full purity) would be **RH itself**, and a
   cohomological framework for *all* of arithmetic — **strictly broader than TPC**. So C7 is a *distinct
   new sibling*, but it is **harder/broader** than C5, not easier. (This is the honest cost of the
   escalation: L-COH buys a *connection to the RH-geometry programs* at the price of routing C5's content
   through a conjecture that subsumes RH.)

2. **C5 re-expressed (the monodromy clause).** The big-monodromy clause (3) is, by Attack P3, the
   **cohomological avatar of C5** — equivalent to or harder than the shift-2 cancellation itself. So part
   of what L-COH "relocates" is C5 wearing sheaves: honest, but **not progress on that clause**.

3. **A THIRD distinct open input: the archimedean place ∞.** Spec ℤ has a place at ∞ with **no
   function-field counterpart** (𝔸^1_{𝔽_q} is "complete" in the relevant sense without an analogue of ∞).
   Every honest transport of a *trace/Lefschetz* formula needs properness/compact support, and ∞ is
   exactly where that breaks: the Connes program treats ∞ separately (e.g. "Weil positivity and trace
   formula, the archimedean place," arXiv:2006.13771), and none of clauses (1)–(3) tells us how ∞ enters
   the trace. So ∞ is a **third located open sub-input**, neither identical to C5 nor reducible to C7's
   bare existence statement — it is a *structural disanalogy* the formalism must absorb. I do **not** call
   it fatal (that would itself be an unaudited terminal claim); I record it as a distinct OPEN input. (No
   step in L-COH handles ∞; asserting that it does would be fabrication.)

**What it found.** The post-transport picture is a **split, not a reduction**: C5's content is dispersed
into (C7) a broader open cohomological-existence conjecture, (cohomological-C5) the monodromy clause that
is C5 in disguise, and (∞) a third distinct open archimedean input. None of the three is *easier* than C5;
two are arguably harder/broader. This is an **escalation** (it links the twin-prime wall to the
Deninger / Connes–Consani / RH-geometry frontier and gives those programs as a new line of attack), and
emphatically **not** a proof and **not** a termination.
