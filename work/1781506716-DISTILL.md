## ADVERSARIAL PASS 1 — the decisive attack: is there a hidden Cesàro handle?

**The attack.** EXPLORE has an interest in concluding "no handle"; I tried hard to FIND
one. The escape shows the FH structure theorem distinguishes genuine-Liouville limits
(strongly stationary) from Sawin's non-Liouville polynomial-phase models. Can that
distinction be levered into anything Cesàro/per-scale about (R2)? I enumerated the three
concrete routes a handle could take and attacked each with the paper in hand.

### Route 1a — use Sawin's own `d`-Fourier uniformity (Def 1.4), which his models DO satisfy, to constrain the `n^{it}` twist at a single scale.

This is the most promising route, because Def 1.4 is a property Sawin's anomalous models
*satisfy* and it is the closest thing in the paper to a per-scale/Cesàro statement. (R2) =
kill the `n^{it}` twist; the twist is a **degree-1 / scaling-flow eigenvalue** object. So:
does vanishing `U^d` (for `d=1`: vanishing `U^1`) force the twist eigenvalue `t=0`?

**Found: NO, and for a structural reason I can state precisely.** Sawin, p. 3 (verbatim,
read from text + confirmed against PDF p. 3): *"The result of Matomäki and Radziwiłł
implies that any Furstenburg limit of λ has vanishing Gowers–Host–Kra `U^1` norm and thus
does not correlate with any 0-step nilsystem."* Def 1.4 with `d=1` is exactly vanishing
`U^1`. Two facts close the route:

1. **The `n^{it}` twist is a scaling-flow eigenvalue** `e^{its}` (n-independent; =1 iff
   `t=0`) — this is the ERG-B content, re-audited below.
2. **A nontrivial eigenfunction has vanishing GHK `U^1` seminorm.** For an eigenfunction
   with eigenvalue `λ=e^{iθ}`, `F(T^h x)=λ^h F(x)`, so the GHK `U^1` statistic
   `E_{h≤H} E_x F(x)\overline{F(T^h x)} = E_{h≤H} λ^{-h} → 0` whenever `λ≠1`. So the twist
   **satisfies** `U^1`-uniformity. Therefore `d=1` Fourier uniformity is **blind** to the
   twist and cannot force `t=0`.
3. Decisively, the `U^1`/`d=1` level is **already a Cesàro theorem** (MRT short intervals,
   Sawin [3], is genuinely Cesàro/short-interval). So whatever per-scale content Sawin's
   `d`-Fourier-uniformity axiom carries is *already available unconditionally at Cesàro* —
   and it provably does not kill the twist. The route delivers nothing new.

### Route 1b — lever the spectral distinction (Sawin's models have irrational spectrum, the genuine λ-limit does not) at the Cesàro level.

**Found: this is the wall, sharpened — not a handle.** "Genuine λ-limit has no irrational
spectrum" is the FH structure theorem ([1]=arXiv:1804.08556, [2]=Annals 187). Upgrading
"no irrational spectrum" to "force the eigenvalue/twist to be *trivial*" is **strong
stationarity**, and FLdlR (arXiv:2304.03121) prove strong stationarity holds for the
**logarithmic** Furstenberg system but **fails for the Cesàro** one. Worse for the
handle: the FLdlR abstract (triangulated below) singles out **Archimedean characters
`n^{it}` as the ONLY pretentious multiplicative functions whose Furstenberg systems have
*trivial rational spectrum***, and a pretentious function has *ergodic* Furstenberg systems
iff it pretends to be a Dirichlet character. The `n^{it}` twist is *literally* an
Archimedean character — the exact extremal object the structure theory carves out as
surviving. So the spectral distinction, pushed to Cesàro, identifies the twist as **the**
obstruction, not as something the structure theory removes. No handle.

### Route 1c — use Sawin's positive Props 4.1/4.2 (assume only Equation 1 = dynamical Chowla) to extract a per-scale `t=0`-forcing statement.

**Found: NO.** Props 4.1/4.2 (pp. 11–12) take **Equation 1** (Chowla, `E_x ∏ F(T^{c_i}x)=0`)
as a *hypothesis* and output sign-pattern existence / counting lower bounds. They are
downstream of exactly what (R2)/C5 is trying to prove and contain no mechanism that forces
a scaling eigenvalue to be trivial. No handle.

**PASS 1 verdict (handed to EXPLORE).** Every route collapses, and now for a *named*
structural reason rather than appeal to FLdlR alone: **the per-scale property Sawin's models
satisfy (Def 1.4 / `d`-Fourier uniformity) is exactly the GHK-seminorm level that is
already unconditionally available at Cesàro (via MRT for `d=1`) and is provably blind to the
`n^{it}` eigenvalue; the only property that forces `t=0` is strong stationarity, which is
log-only (FLdlR).** I did **not** find a Cesàro handle, partial or otherwise. The wall holds
and is, if anything, sharper: the audit pins *why* the per-scale level cannot reach the
twist (eigenfunctions are `U^1`-uniform). This is **not** progress on (R2)/C5.

## ADVERSARIAL PASS 3 — conflation check / the axiom identity

**The attack.** Pin down a possible internal tension. EXPLORE says the *dropped first axiom
of [7=Tao, Prop 1]* is "the dynamical system actually arises as a limit of Liouville," yet
also says the escape is bought by "axiom (i) = **strong stationarity**." Are these the same
property? Does "arises-as-limit-of-Liouville ⟹ strong stationarity (log)"? Or is the
argument conflating Sawin's Def 1.1 axioms (1)–(5), Sawin's Def 1.4 `d`-Fourier uniformity,
and Tao's Prop-1 axioms (i)–(iv)?

**Found: NO conflation. The logical dependency the EXPLORE step relies on is sound, with one
nuance worth recording.** Three distinct conditions are in play and the step keeps them
distinct:

- **Sawin Def 1.1 axioms (1)–(5)** (PDF p. 2, audited): the algebraic identities defining a
  *dynamical model for λ* (`nM(In x)=M(x)`, `M(Tx)=M(x)+1`, `T(In x)=In(T^n x)`,
  `Im(In x)=Inm(x)`, `F(In x)=λ(n)F(x)`). Sawin's anomalous models satisfy these. These are
  NOT strong stationarity and NOT `d`-Fourier uniformity.
- **Sawin Def 1.4 `d`-Fourier uniformity** (PDF p. 3, audited): vanishing GHK `U^d` seminorm
  of `F`. This is the *weaker replacement* for Tao's axiom (i). Sawin's anomalous models
  satisfy this. Strong stationarity is strictly finer (see below).
- **Tao Prop-1 axiom (i)** (Sawin p. 2, verbatim): *"the dynamical system actually arises as
  a limit of Liouville."* Remark 3.5 (PDF p. 11, audited): after the inverse-limit + affine
  action, a dynamical model satisfies Tao's **(ii),(iii),(iv)** and "**may or may not satisfy
  axiom (i)**." So (i) is precisely the separating condition.

**Are "arises as a limit of Liouville" (axiom (i)) and "strong stationarity" the same?**
They are not *literally identical*, but the dependency `axiom (i) ⟹ strong stationarity` —
the only direction the argument uses — is **correct and now independently audited**, with
the crucial qualifier that it holds **at the logarithmic level**:

1. **Strong stationarity = dilation invariance, independently confirmed.** Furstenberg–
   Katznelson's definition (search-surfaced, ResearchGate "Structure of Strongly Stationary
   Systems"; Springer J. Anal. Math.): a measure is strongly stationary iff invariant under
   the dilation `τ_r` for **every** `r∈ℕ` — equivalently `∫∏S^{j} = ∫∏S^{rj}` for all `r`.
   This is exactly the `n↦pn` dilation invariance ERG-B/ERG-1 name. It is a **different and
   strictly finer** property than vanishing `U^d` (Def 1.4). So Def 1.4 ≠ strong
   stationarity — **the step did not conflate them.** (Confirmed concretely in PASS 1: the
   twist *satisfies* `U^1` but *violates* dilation-triviality unless `t=0`.)
2. **Axiom (i) at the log level supplies strong stationarity.** FLdlR (arXiv:2304.03121,
   triangulated PASS 4): *when the Furstenberg system is built by **logarithmic** averaging,
   trivial rational spectrum implies strong stationarity; this fails for **Cesàro**.* A
   genuine *limit of Liouville* in Tao's sense (axiom (i)) is built by **logarithmic
   averaging** — confirmed verbatim from Sawin himself, PDF p. 2 (audited): *"By
   **logarithmically averaging** the point measures associated to numbers n in the interval
   [1,…,N], we can define a measure on X."* So the genuine λ-limit (axiom (i)) is a
   log-averaged object, for which trivial-rational-spectrum ⟹ strong stationarity. The
   chain `axiom (i) ⟹ (log-averaged limit of λ) ⟹ strong stationarity` is therefore sound,
   and its log-only character is **internal to Sawin's own construction**, not imported.
3. **Hence the "log-only" disposition is correct.** The escape from Sawin's counterexample
   uses axiom (i); axiom (i)'s force is strong stationarity; strong stationarity is a
   log-averaging phenomenon (FLdlR + Sawin p. 2); (R2) is Cesàro, where it provably fails.

**Nuance recorded (not a flaw, but EXPLORE should weight it).** EXPLORE's phrase "axiom (i)
= strong stationarity" is a slight *shorthand*: precisely, axiom (i) is "arises as a
log-limit of Liouville," and strong stationarity is the *structural consequence* (via
FLdlR's trivial-spectrum-⟹-strong-stationarity, log-only) that does the `t=0`-forcing work.
The equation sign is an abbreviation, not an identity; the load-bearing implication
`axiom (i) ⟹ strong stationarity (log)` is what matters and it holds. No correction
required to the verdict; the wording could be tightened to "axiom (i) *supplies* strong
stationarity (at the log level)." **No conflation; dependencies are correct.**
