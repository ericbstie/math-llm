## ATTACK 1 — Is clause (i) a tautology? (the leverage test)

**Claim under attack.** `C5 ⟺ sup_{d≤x^{1−ε}} A^(2)_d·R_d → 0` is offered as a
"categorical characterization / Beck–Chevalley enriched-iso form."

**Attack.** Strip the categorical dress. The factorization `U_d = A^(2)_d·R_d` is, by the
definitions above, literally
```
‖S_{·,d}‖_∞/(x/d)  =  [ (‖S_{·,d}‖_2/√d)/(x/d) ] · [ ‖S_{·,d}‖_∞ / (‖S_{·,d}‖_2/√d) ].
```
The two bracketed factors multiply to `‖S_{·,d}‖_∞/(x/d)` by cancelling
`‖S_{·,d}‖_2/√d`. This is the trivial identity `‖v‖_∞ = (‖v‖_2/√d)·(‖v‖_∞/(‖v‖_2/√d))`

the moduli fibration is used. The "iff" is then `sup_d U_d → 0 ⟺ sup_d U_d → 0`.

### 2b — THE STRUCTURAL KILL: what is `Σ_a S_{a,d}²`, and what does L² control actually deliver?

### 2c — Does variance / large-sieve / Bombieri–Vinogradov machinery reach this object? (literature)

The hope behind any "L² is easier" reduction is that variance of `Σ_{n≡a(d)} f(n)` is
controlled unconditionally by the **large sieve via orthogonality of Dirichlet
characters** — which requires `f` **multiplicative**, so that `Σ_n f(n)χ(n)` is an
L-function-type sum with its own cancellation, and the variance collapses to a small
diagonal. Searches:

- E. Kowalski / standard treatments: "the classical multiplicative large sieve encodes
  the **quasi-orthogonality of Dirichlet characters** of varying moduli" — the mechanism
  is intrinsically about *multiplicative* `f` (search: "Demystifying the arithmetic large
  sieve", and Tao 254A Notes 3 on large sieve / Bombieri–Vinogradov).
- Bombieri–Vinogradov-for-multiplicative-functions work (Granville–Harper–Soundararajan
  and successors) extends BV precisely to **multiplicative** `f`; the variance/correlation
  applications all run through multiplicativity (search: "Bombieri–Vinogradov for
  multiplicative functions").
- **Matomäki–Radziwiłł–Tao / Tao 2015 (arXiv:1509.05422)** prove the **logarithmically
  averaged** two-point Chowla using "**multiplicativity of the Liouville function at small
  primes**" + the **entropy decrement argument** + averaging over scales. Crucially `t(n)
  = λ(n)λ(n+2)` is **not** multiplicative (the +2 shift kills it: `t(nm) ≠ t(n)t(m)`), so
  there is **no character orthogonality for `t` itself**; the proofs reach it only after
  averaging restores enough multiplicative structure.

**This is exactly what TEST 2b exhibits structurally.** When you apply the variance
method to the non-multiplicative `t`, the off-diagonal does **not** collapse to a
negligible error via orthogonality; it returns the **same family of shifted correlations
of `t`** (at shifts `kd`). The shift has destroyed the multiplicativity that, for a
genuine multiplicative `f` (the `t_mult` column in 2a), is what would let the large sieve
give a real, unconditional variance bound. Here the variance is *carried by the trivial
diagonal* and the *nontrivial* part (the off-diagonal, and the sup) is just more C5.

**On why averaging cannot be removed (the averaged→uniform gap, from the source).** Tao's
own account of arXiv:1509.05422 (search-surfaced quote): removing the log-averaging to
reach **unaveraged** Chowla "requires one to prove **expansion properties of a certain
random graph** ... Unfortunately, [we] were unable to establish such an expansion
property, as the edges in the relevant graph **do not seem to be either random enough or
structured enough** for standard methods." And: "**the only part of the argument that
requires the logarithmic averaging** is [this implication]." The averaging is over
scales/moduli — it is *exactly* the L∞-in-`d` (single large `d`) information that `R_d`
at the edge demands and that the method provably does not supply. This is the literature
confirmation that the averaged↔uniform gap is real and currently unbridged, and that it
is **not** a packaging artifact removable by reorganization.

## ATTACK 3 — Clause (ii), the content-locating claim

`A^(2)_d ≈ √(d/x)`), with no reduction in difficulty. I record clause (ii)'s
