## ADVERSARIAL PASS 1 — Stress-test 3: Is FH "no irrational spectrum" genuinely LOG-ONLY?

**The attack.** Leak 1 (the foundation of the whole "rigidity can't see the per-scale twist"
verdict) claims the FH structure theorem lives in the log-averaged / `t=0` regime and "cannot remove
the `dn/n` measure." If instead FH had an un-log-averaged (Cesàro) version of the `t=0`-forcing
content, then (R2)'s residual would shrink and there would be a genuine handle. I searched
specifically for any Cesàro / un-averaged version of the FH–Lemańczyk structure theorem.

**What I found (search-surfaced, near-verbatim, concordant across 4 independent queries).**

(a) The FH structure theorem *itself* ("no irrational spectrum; building blocks are Bernoulli +
infinite-step nilsystems") is stated for systems *"naturally associated with any finite collection
of multiplicative functions that take values on the complex unit disc"* — i.e. it is **not, by its
statement, restricted to logarithmic averaging** (IMRN 2021 abstract; arXiv:1804.08556).

(b) But the **Sarnak/Chowla *consequences* are explicitly logarithmic**:
> *"strongly aperiodic multiplicative functions satisfy the **logarithmically averaged** variant of
> the disjointness conjecture of Sarnak..."* (IMRN 2021 abstract, verbatim-level)
> *"Local Gowers uniformity of the Liouville function is equivalent to the function having all
> Gowers–Host–Kra seminorms vanishing in every Furstenberg limit **with logarithmic averaging**."*
> (search summary of Tao's blog "Furstenberg limits of the Liouville function")

(c) The **decisive** fact — the exact load-bearing mechanism — surfaced verbatim-level from
FH–Lemańczyk arXiv:2304.03121 across three separate queries:
> *"When Furstenberg systems are obtained using **logarithmic** averages, **trivial rational
> spectrum implies a strong dilation invariance property called strong stationarity**, but **this
> property fails when the systems are obtained using Cesàro averages**."*
> *"In the case of Cesàro averages, trivial rational spectrum does **not** always imply ... strong
> stationarity, **contrasting a result for logarithmic averages that holds for all bounded
> multiplicative functions**."*

(d) And the target invariant is itself only-logarithmic-proven:
> *"The unaveraged Chowla conjecture (two-point) ... `∑_{n≤x} λ(a₁n+b₁)λ(a₂n+b₂)=o(x)` ... remains
> **open**."* Only the logarithmic version (Tao arXiv:1509.05422) is proven; **even the Cesàro
> two-point Chowla is open** (confirmed verbatim, consistent with the graph's C5 sub-facts).

**Verdict on the attack — WALL HELD, with a correction to Leak 1's statement.**
The handle does **not** open: there is **no un-log-averaged version of the `t=0`-forcing content**.
On the contrary, the literature states *explicitly* that the property that would force the twist away
(strong stationarity = dilation invariance under `n↦pn`, exactly the `dn/n`-symmetry) **provably
fails at the Cesàro level**. So the un-averaging step `L-ERG-RIG` isolates is a **documented gap**,
not a missing-but-approachable theorem.

**BUT the attack also caught an imprecision in EXPLORE's Leak 1 that EXPLORE must fix when
labelling.** Leak 1 said *"the FH 'no irrational spectrum' theorem is about the log-averaged system;
it already lives in the `t=0` world."* That is **slightly mis-stated**: the *structure theorem* (no
irrational spectrum, nil+Bernoulli building blocks) is **not** intrinsically log-only — by its own
abstract it applies to Furstenberg systems of multiplicative functions in general. What is genuinely
**log-only** is the **`t=0`-forcing consequence**: the implication *trivial-rational-spectrum ⇒
strong stationarity ⇒ (the dilation invariance that pins the scaling-flow eigenvalue)*. The honest,
sharper, citation-backed Leak 1 is therefore:

> **Leak 1 (corrected).** "No irrational spectrum" alone does **not** force the per-scale
> archimedean eigenvalue `t(X)` to 0 — an `n^{it}` factor is a *rational-spectrum-trivial /
> Kronecker* object (Part B: it is the scaling-flow character `e^{itu}`), and FH/Lemańczyk show such
> a factor is admissible. The step that would kill it is **strong stationarity (dilation
> invariance)**, and FH–Lemańczyk prove strong stationarity holds for the **logarithmic** Furstenberg
> system but **fails for Cesàro** systems. Hence the per-scale / un-averaged killing of the `n^{it}`
> twist is exactly the **Cesàro-vs-log strong-stationarity gap** — a gap the literature names and
> leaves open, presupposing the `dn/n` measure (= the prior step's (M1) dilation invariance).

This is *stronger* than what EXPLORE wrote: the wall now has a **named theorem-level reason**
(strong stationarity is log-only) and an explicit literature statement that the Cesàro analogue
**fails**, not merely "is unproven." **No handle. Wall held, sharper.**

## ADVERSARIAL PASS 2 — Stress-test 4: Is Sawin's class genuinely CLOSED under the FH/Lemańczyk structure theorems? (the decisive Leak-2 claim)

**The attack.** Leak 2 is the load-bearing negative claim: *no class-invariant structure theorem can

- *"**Most recent progress on the Chowla conjectures and sign patterns** of the Möbius and Liouville

## ADVERSARIAL PASS 3 — Stress-test 5: Can ANY higher-rank action let Ratner/EKL/×2×3/BLMV re-enter?

**The attack (the most promising place for a genuine handle).** Leak 3 says genuine measure rigidity
is inapplicable because the system is a positive-entropy single-`ℤ` action with no higher-rank
algebraic structure. The sharpest counter EXPLORE itself flagged: **the multiplicative structure of
`λ` (the dilation `λ(pn)=−λ(n)`) might supply a `×p, ×q` commuting / adelic higher-rank action** —
exactly the kind Furstenberg `×2,×3` / Rudolph / Einsiedler–Lindenstrauss rigidity needs. If a
genuine higher-rank action whose invariant measure encodes `t(X)` exists, rigidity (which forces
Lebesgue from positive entropy under higher rank) could bite. I tested this construction concretely.

**What measure rigidity actually requires (search-surfaced, verbatim-level).**
> *"Rudolph showed that for relatively prime positive integers, if a `×n, ×m`-ergodic measure has
> **positive entropy** with respect to one of these maps, then it is **Lebesgue measure**."*
> *"Einsiedler and Lindenstrauss proved that for [higher-rank `ℤ^d`, `d>1`] actions, if an
> α-invariant ergodic measure has positive entropy ..., then the measure is Lebesgue."*

So the rigidity engine needs: **(R-i)** a genuine higher-rank (`d≥2`) action by *commuting* maps,
**(R-ii)** those maps must *preserve the same measure* (be a joint action on one system), **(R-iii)**
positive entropy for one map — then it concludes Lebesgue (maximal entropy), which would indeed kill
any extra eigenvalue structure including the `n^{it}` twist.

**The concrete construction and why it FAILS — the `×p` dilation is not a measure-preserving
automorphism of the Furstenberg system.** The candidate higher-rank action is `{D_p : D_p f(n) =
f(pn)}` for primes `p` (commuting: `D_p D_q = D_q D_p = D_{pq}`). I check whether `D_p` is an
*automorphism of the Furstenberg dynamical system of `λ`*, i.e. whether it preserves the Furstenberg
measure `μ` together with the shift `T`. Two obstructions, both decisive:

*Obstruction A (`D_p` does not commute with the shift `T`; it is not even an endomorphism of the
`ℤ`-system).* The shift is `T: (x_n) ↦ (x_{n+1})`; dilation is `D_p: (x_n) ↦ (x_{pn})`. Then
`D_p T (x)_n = x_{p(n+1)} = x_{pn+p}` whereas `T D_p (x)_n = x_{pn+1}`. So `D_p T = T^p D_p`, **not**
`T D_p`. `D_p` intertwines `T` with `T^p` — it is a *factor map to a `p`-fold rescaled system*, not a
symmetry of the single `ℤ`-action. The pair `(T, D_p)` does **not** generate a `ℤ^2`-action by
commuting transformations of one system; it generates the `ax+b`-type **affine semigroup**
`n ↦ pn+b` — exactly the *non-invertible, single-system-on-each-scale* "semigroup of affine maps"
the literature attaches to the **logarithmic** Furstenberg limit (PASS 1, fact (b)), **not** a
higher-rank diagonalizable action.

*Obstruction B (it lives at the log level anyway, and is not measure-preserving at the Cesàro
level).* The dilation `D_p` is exactly the strong-stationarity / dilation-invariance symmetry — and
PASS 1 established (citation-backed) that this symmetry **holds only for the logarithmic system and
fails for Cesàro**. So even the *semigroup* structure `D_p` is a `dn/n`-measure artifact; at the
per-scale / Cesàro level (which is where (R2) must be killed) `D_p` is **not** a measure-preserving
map of the Furstenberg system. There is therefore **no** measure on which both a higher-rank action
and the per-scale twist coexist for rigidity to act on.

**Numerical sanity check that `D_p T = T^p D_p` (not `T D_p`) on actual Liouville data** (run ad hoc):

```python

lam[1] = 1
```

automorphisms of a single measure-preserving system. Rigidity theorems (Rudolph/EKL/Furstenberg

**Verdict on the attack — WALL HELD, decisively, with the obstruction made concrete.** There is no
higher-rank algebraic action to which Ratner/EKL/×2×3/BLMV apply:
- The multiplicative dilations `D_p` are the **affine semigroup** (the `ax+b` structure), **not** a
  commuting toral/diagonalizable `ℤ^d` action; `D_p∘T = T^p∘D_p ≠ T∘D_p` (verified numerically).
- That semigroup is a **logarithmic-system artifact** (strong stationarity, log-only per PASS 1); it
  is **not measure-preserving at the Cesàro/per-scale level** where (R2) must be killed.
- The genuine-rigidity engine concludes *Lebesgue/maximal-entropy* from positive entropy **under
  higher rank**; with only rank 1 (single `ℤ`) and positive entropy there is a vast unclassifiable
  measure space — rigidity is vacuous, exactly Leak 3.
- An adelic / `×p` reformulation does not rescue this: the adelic dilation action's invariant
  measure that one would feed to rigidity is again the **dilation-invariant (`dn/n`) / log** object,
  i.e. it re-imports the very averaging (R2) is trying to remove. **No handle.** The one creative
  route EXPLORE flagged is concretely closed.
