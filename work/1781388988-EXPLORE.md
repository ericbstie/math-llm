## PART 1 — GROUNDING: the exact chain of inputs in the 𝔽_q[t] proof, and what ℤ lacks

I lay out the mechanism as four load-bearing inputs (a)–(d), each cited at the level I can verify
from primary sources, with the ℤ-analogue's status attached.

### Setup (the dictionary, stated precisely)

Over 𝔽_q[t]: monic polynomials of degree n ↔ integers near x = q^n; the **norm** |f| = q^{deg f}
↔ size; **irreducible monic polynomials** ↔ primes; the **von Mangoldt** Λ(f) and **Möbius/Liouville**
μ(f), λ(f) are defined identically (λ(f) = (−1)^{# irreducible factors with multiplicity}). "Twin
primes" = pairs of monic irreducibles (f, f+a) with a ∈ 𝔽_q^× fixed (the shift "2" becomes a fixed
nonzero constant; the relevant correlation is ∑_{deg f = n} λ(f) λ(f+a), the exact analogue of
∑_{n≤x} λ(n) λ(n+2)). The number field ↔ function field analogy is the standard one (Weil; Iwaniec–
Kowalski Ch. on function fields): both Spec ℤ and the affine line 𝔸^1_{𝔽_q} = Spec 𝔽_q[t] are
1-dimensional arithmetic schemes; ζ_{𝔽_q[t]}(s) = 1/(1 − q^{1−s}) is the function-field zeta, with
**RH proved** (Weil) — this is the structural disanalogy with ℤ, where RH is open.

### Input (a) — Frobenius + Grothendieck–Lefschetz: the correlation IS a trace

In the 𝔽_q[t] world, an arithmetic sum over monic f of degree n is converted into a **geometric
count of 𝔽_q-points** of a variety/space parametrizing those f, and then — via the
**Grothendieck–Lefschetz trace formula** — into an *alternating sum of traces of the geometric
Frobenius* Frob_q acting on **ℓ-adic étale cohomology** H^i_c of the relevant moduli space with
coefficients in a constructible sheaf 𝓕 built from λ (a "Möbius/Kummer-type" sheaf):
$$ \sum_{\deg f = n} \lambda(f)\lambda(f+a) \;=\; \sum_i (-1)^i \,\mathrm{Tr}\!\big(\mathrm{Frob}_q^{\,n} \,\big|\, H^i_c(X_{\overline{\mathbb F_q}}, \mathcal F)\big). $$
The Liouville function over 𝔽_q[t] is itself the trace function of a (virtual) lisse sheaf, and the
shift f ↦ f+a is an algebraic map, so the *correlation* is again a trace function. **This is the
input ℤ structurally lacks**: there is no known scheme over a finite field whose Frobenius-point-count
is ∑_{n≤x} λ(n)λ(n+2), because Spec ℤ has **no absolute Frobenius** and **no base finite field**.
(Citations: Grothendieck–Lefschetz trace formula — Grothendieck, SGA 4½/SGA 5; exposition Katz,
*L-functions and Monodromy: Four Lectures on Weil II*, Princeton lecture notes, §1. Sawin–Shusterman
arXiv:1808.04001 set up exactly such sheaf-theoretic correlations.)

### Input (b) — Deligne purity / RH-over-finite-fields: the square-root cancellation = the weight

Each Frobenius eigenvalue on H^i_c(X, 𝓕) of a **pure sheaf of weight w** has absolute value
q^{(w+i)/2} (Deligne, **Weil II**, *Publ. IHÉS* 52 (1980)). When the relevant cohomology is
concentrated in the expected degree and the sheaf is pure, this gives the **square-root cancellation**:
|∑ λ(f)λ(f+a)| ≪ (number of terms)^{1/2} times a bounded number of eigenvalues — i.e. the
**o(main term)** strength one needs. The "weight" is exactly the exponent in this square-root bound.
**This is the function-field RH**, and it is a *theorem* there (Deligne). Its ℤ-analogue is an
**RH-type statement** (a purity/weight bound for whatever cohomology would compute the integer
correlation) and is itself **open**; worse, see Part 2.4 — even *full* RH/GRH is provably **not
sufficient** for the two-point λ-correlation. (Citation: Deligne, "La conjecture de Weil II,"
*Publ. Math. IHÉS* **52** (1980), 137–252; exposition Katz, Weil II lectures; Michel, "Diophantine
consequences of Deligne's Weil II.")

### Input (c) — a *big* geometric monodromy group (Katz-style): forces vanishing to main order

Purity (b) gives square-root cancellation *only if the cohomology does not concentrate in too low a
degree and the top-degree (invariant) part vanishes*. That vanishing is controlled by the
**geometric monodromy group** G_geom of the family — the Zariski closure of the image of the étale
fundamental group π_1^{geom} acting on the sheaf. **Katz's method** (Katz, *Gauss Sums, Kloosterman
Sums, and Monodromy Groups*, Ann. Math. Studies 116; and the Weil II lectures) is to **prove
G_geom is as large as possible** (e.g. a full classical group), which by representation theory forces
the space of monodromy **co/invariants to vanish**, hence kills the would-be main term and leaves
only the pure lower-weight error — i.e. forces the correlation to vanish to main order. **The actual
twin-prime input in Sawin–Shusterman is a function-field analogue of a Fouvry–Michel exponential-sum
estimate**, proved by exactly this big-monodromy route, yielding a **level of distribution beyond
1/2** for irreducible polynomials (their abstract: "a function field variant of a result by
Fouvry–Michel on exponential sums involving the Möbius function … level of distribution beyond 1/2
… twin prime conjecture in a quantitative form"). Equivalently, the Möbius function over 𝔽_q[T],
restricted to certain special subspaces, **can be mimicked by Dirichlet characters** (their phrasing)
— the geometric statement that drives the cancellation. **ℤ-analogue: ABSENT.** There is no
fundamental group / monodromy group acting on a cohomology of Spec ℤ whose largeness is known and
forces the integer correlation to cancel; this is conjectural at every link. (Citations:
Sawin–Shusterman arXiv:1808.04001, abstract + Fouvry–Michel input; Katz GKM and Weil II; Fouvry–
Michel on μ-twisted exponential sums.)

### Input (d) — the formalism it all runs in: six functors + duality (= the functional equation)

The above is not ad hoc: it lives in the **six-functor formalism** (f^*, f_*, f_!, f^!, ⊗, RHom) on
ℓ-adic sheaves, with **Poincaré–Verdier duality** providing the symmetry H^i_c ↔ (H^{2d−i})^∨ that is
the cohomological avatar of the **functional equation** of the L-function, and the **Lefschetz trace
/ Euler–Poincaré formula** computing dimensions and the leading behavior. Purity is stable under the
six operations (Deligne), which is what lets one *propagate* weight bounds through the geometric
constructions (pushforward along the "shift and multiply" maps). **ℤ-analogue: CONJECTURAL/ABSENT** —
there is no established Weil-type cohomology with a six-functor formalism and a Poincaré-duality
functional equation **over Spec ℤ** (or over 𝔽_1). Constructing one is precisely the goal of the
Deninger and Connes–Consani programs (Part 2).
