## Part 2 — The object, and a correction to the first formulation

The first formulation used `g_z(n) = z^Omega(n)`. **That was wrong** and is recorded as such: `2^Omega(n)`
is *not* `d(n)` (they differ on prime powers; first disagreement at n=4), so the "solved point" claimed
for it did not exist. The correct family is the generalized divisor function

    zeta(s)^z = sum_n d_z(n) n^{-s},     d_z multiplicative,  d_z(p^a) = binom(z+a-1, a).

Define the **two-variable shifted correlation**

    T(x; z, w) = sum_{n <= x} d_z(n) d_w(n+2).

Four marked points, all verified computationally in Part 4:

| point of `T` | is |
|---|---|
| `d/dz d/dw` at `(0,0)` | **TPC** — because `d/dz|_0 d_z(n) = Lambda(n)/log n` |
| `(2,2)` | `sum d(n)d(n+2)` — **Estermann / Motohashi, solved with power saving** |
| `(z,2)`, all complex `z` | **Drappeau–Topacogullari — SOLVED** (see Part 5) |
| `(-1,-1)` | `sum mu(n)mu(n+2)` — 2-point Chowla for `mu` |

**Stated gap (do not paper over):** the last row is Chowla for **mu**, not the project's `C5`, which is for
**lambda**. `d_{-1} = mu` exactly (verified), and `lambda` is NOT `d_z` for any `z` (its Dirichlet series
is `zeta(2s)/zeta(s)`, not a power of `zeta`). A23 therefore does **not** contain `C5` as a literal point.

## Part 3 — Candidate lemma `L-SS-ORIGIN`

Heuristic main term, product-of-means normalisation:

    T(x; z, w)  ~  S(z,w) * [x (log x)^{z-1} / Gamma(z)] * [(log x)^{w-1} / Gamma(w)]

with `S(z,w) = prod_p sigma_p(z,w)`, `sigma_p = A_p / (1 - 1/p)^{2-z-w}`, and `A_p` the local average of
`d_z(n) d_w(n+2)`. For odd `p`, at most one of `n, n+2` is divisible by `p`, giving the closed form

    A_p = (1 - 2/p) + (1 - 1/p) * [ (1-1/p)^{-z} + (1-1/p)^{-w} - 2 ].

**`L-SS-ORIGIN`.** *`S(0,0) = 2*Pi_2`, the Hardy–Littlewood twin-prime constant, exactly.*

*Proof (one line, then verified numerically).* `d_0(1) = 1` and `d_0(p^a) = 0` for `a >= 1`, since
`zeta^0 = 1`. Hence for odd `p` only the `(0,0)`-valuation term survives: `A_p = 1 - 2/p`, so
`sigma_p = (1-2/p)/(1-1/p)^2` — **the Hardy–Littlewood local factor**. At `p = 2`, only `n` odd
contributes, so `A_2 = 1/2` and `sigma_2 = (1/2)/(1/2)^2 = 2` — **the Hardy–Littlewood factor at 2**.
Therefore `S(0,0) = 2 * prod_{p>2} (1-2/p)/(1-1/p)^2 = 2*Pi_2`. ∎

**Consequence (the reason this matters).** `1/(Gamma(z)Gamma(w)) = zw(1 + O(z,w))`, so applying
`d/dz d/dw` at the origin to the main term returns exactly `S(0,0) * x/(log x)^2`. The naive
Selberg–Delange main term, continued to the origin, **reproduces the Hardy–Littlewood conjecture
including its constant**.

> **Therefore the parity obstruction is not located in the main term.** It cannot be: the main term
> already carries the correct answer. Parity must live entirely in (a) the legitimacy of the joint
> analytic continuation in `(z,w)`, or (b) the uniformity of the error term as `(z,w) -> (0,0)`.

This is a **localization**, not a bound, not progress toward TPC, and not a foundation.

## Part 5 — K1 fired partially; novelty NOT established

- **Drappeau–Topacogullari** have full asymptotic expansions for shifted convolutions involving
  `tau_z`, `z` complex, against `d(n+h)`. **The line `w = 2` is solved for all complex `z`.** This does not
  kill A23 — it *sharpens* it: the gate is moving `w` off 2 toward 0, and the mechanism for why that is
  hard is identifiable (at `w = 2` the binary additive divisor problem supplies Klousterman/spectral
  input; `d_w(n+2)` for general `w` does not).
- **Conrey–Keating**, *Moments of zeta and correlations of divisor-sums* I–V, work with `d_k(n)d_k(n+h)`
  and Hardy–Littlewood together as interchangeable inputs. **`L-SS-ORIGIN` is therefore plausibly
  folklore in that circle**, and the *reasoning direction* there is the reverse of A23's (they assume HL
  to compute divisor correlations).
- **Matomäki–Radziwiłł–Tao**, *Correlations of the von Mangoldt and higher divisor functions*, occupy the
  `(z, k)` region rigorously.

**NOVELTY IS NOT CLAIMED FOR ANY PART OF A23.** The mathematics in Part 3 is verified; whether the
observation is new is unresolved and must be settled by reading Conrey–Keating I–V and
Drappeau–Topacogullari in full before any further claim is made. *(Recorded in direct response to the
A22 failure earlier in this campaign, where an unchecked novelty claim did not survive DISTILL.)*
