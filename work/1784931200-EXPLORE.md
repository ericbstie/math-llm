## Part 1 — Why this line, and what is genuinely new about it

Every approach node from A1 to A21 attacks the same axis: **parity** (Trap B), localized at **C5**. The
campaign's own meta-pattern — *Trap A is dodgeable, Trap B is universal* — was established by pushing
five successive out-of-framework imports (A16 ergodic, A18 affine, A19 cyclic, A20 δ-geometry, A21
from-scratch) against λ. **A22 does not attack C5 and does not mention λ.**

Its premise is that the difficulty of TPC decomposes into **two independent obstructions**, and the
project has only ever named one:

1. **Parity (Trap B).** Sieve-type data cannot fix the parity of Ω. This is C5's home.
2. **Specificity (proposed: "Trap C").** Even granting an oracle for "which even numbers occur as prime
   gaps", nothing forces the *particular* number **2**.

The second axis is invisible from inside the C5 framing because C5 is *already* shift-2-specific. It
becomes visible only when one looks at the family of results that deliberately abstracts away from the
individual shift — the **largeness / Ramsey-theoretic program on the Polignac set**, which is an
**active 2024–2026 literature line explicitly motivated by TPC** and which this repository has never
recorded.

**Definition.** Let
\[
\mathcal{D} \;=\; \{\, h \in 2\mathbb{N} \;:\; h = p'-p \ \text{for infinitely many prime pairs} \,\}
\]
(the *Polignac numbers*; the weak/strong distinction — differences of arbitrary vs. consecutive primes —
is immaterial to everything below). TPC is exactly the statement \(2 \in \mathcal{D}\).

**Known (literature).**
- \(\mathcal{D} \neq \emptyset\) — Zhang; Maynard; Polymath8.
- \(\mathcal{D}\) has **positive lower density** and **bounded gaps** — Pintz, arXiv:1305.6289.
- \(\mathcal{D}\) is an **IP\*** set — Banks, arXiv:2403.10637 *(OPEN-capped)*.
- \(\mathcal{D}\) is **multiplicatively syndetic** — Goswami, arXiv:2406.02243 *(OPEN-capped)*.

The program's direction of travel is clear: prove ever-stronger largeness for \(\mathcal{D}\), hoping to
squeeze out \(2\). **A22's finding is that this is provably impossible**, for a reason that has nothing
to do with parity.

## Part 2 — Candidate lemma `L-PIGEON` (the quantitative no-go)

The most natural route from Maynard–Tao to a *specific* gap is pigeonholing. Maynard–Tao gives: for every
\(m\) there is \(k=k(m)\) such that every admissible \(k\)-tuple \(H\) has, infinitely often, at least
\(m\) of \(n+h_1,\dots,n+h_k\) prime. Pigeonhole a fixed \(m\)-subset \(S \subseteq H\) that works
infinitely often; then \(S-S \subseteq \mathcal{D}\cup\{0\}\). To force \(h \in \mathcal{D}\) one wants
every \(m\)-subset of \(H\) to contain two elements at distance \(h\).

**`L-PIGEON`.** *Let \(H \subset \mathbb{Z}\) be finite, \(|H| = k\), and \(h \neq 0\). Let \(G_h(H)\) be
the graph on \(H\) with \(a \sim b\) iff \(|a-b| = h\). Then:*

**(i)** \(G_h(H)\) *is the induced subgraph on \(H\) of the Cayley graph of \((\mathbb{Z},+)\) with
generator \(h\). That Cayley graph is a disjoint union of \(|h|\) bi-infinite paths (one per residue class
mod \(h\)), so it is acyclic; hence \(G_h(H)\) is a disjoint union of finite paths.*

**(ii)** *Consequently* \(\alpha(G_h(H)) \ge \lceil k/2 \rceil\), *since a path on \(t\) vertices has
independence number \(\lceil t/2\rceil\) and \(\sum \lceil t_i/2 \rceil \ge \lceil (\sum t_i)/2 \rceil\).*

**(iii)** *For \(h=2\) the bound is attained structurally: \(\{a,a+2,a+4\}\) meets all three residues mod
3, so an admissible \(H\) (with \(k\ge3\)) contains no 3-term chain in steps of 2. Every component of
\(G_2(H)\) therefore has at most 2 vertices — \(G_2(H)\) is a **matching**.*

**(iv) Consequence.** *The pigeonhole route can force a specific gap \(h\) only if \(m > \alpha(G_h(H))
\ge k/2\). Maynard (Ann. of Math. **181** (2015)) requires \(k \gg m^2 e^{4m}\), i.e. \(m \asymp \log k\).
The route is short by an exponential factor and can never force gap 2 — for any tuple, any \(m\).*

Note (ii) needs **no admissibility hypothesis at all**; (iii) merely shows the bound is sharp rather than
lossy. The obstruction is therefore **purely combinatorial and unconditional** — it is not a parity
phenomenon and would survive an oracle for C5.

## Part 3 — Candidate lemma `L-SPEC` (the structural no-go — the main claim)

**Definition.** A family \(\mathsf{L}\) of subsets of \(\mathbb{N}\) is **finitely stable** if
\(A \in \mathsf{L}\) and \(F\) finite imply \(A \setminus F \in \mathsf{L}\).

**`L-SPEC`(a) — the duality lemma.** *Let \(\mathcal{P}\) be a partition-regular family of subsets of
\(\mathbb{N}\) all of whose members are infinite, and let \(\mathsf{L} = \mathcal{P}^*\) be its dual
(\(A \in \mathsf{L}\) iff \(A \cap P \neq \emptyset\) for every \(P \in \mathcal{P}\)). Then \(\mathsf{L}\)
is finitely stable.*

*Proof.* Let \(A \in \mathsf{L}\) and \(P \in \mathcal{P}\). Two-colour \(P\) by membership in \(A\). By
partition regularity there is a monochromatic \(P' \in \mathcal{P}\) with \(P' \subseteq P\). If
\(P' \subseteq A^c\) then \(A \cap P' = \emptyset\), contradicting \(A \in \mathsf{L}\). So
\(P' \subseteq A\), and \(A \cap P \supseteq P'\) is **infinite**. Hence for any finite \(F\),
\((A\setminus F) \cap P \neq \emptyset\). As \(P\) was arbitrary, \(A \setminus F \in \mathsf{L}\). ∎

**`L-SPEC`(b) — instances.** The lemma applies to
- **IP\*** (IP sets are partition regular by **Hindman's theorem**; every IP set is infinite),
- **Δ\*** (Δ-sets are partition regular by **Ramsey's theorem**; every Δ-set is infinite),
- **central\*** (central sets are partition regular; every central set contains an IP set, hence infinite).

**`L-SPEC`(c) — direct instances.** Finite stability also holds, by elementary argument, for
- **syndetic** (gaps \(\le N\); deleting \(|F|\) points gives gaps \(\le (|F|+1)N\)),
- **piecewise syndetic**, **positive upper Banach density** (immediate),
- **multiplicatively syndetic** (if \(\bigcup_{t\in F_0} t^{-1}A = \mathbb{N}\), deleting \(a\) uncovers at
  most the \(\le |F_0|\) points \(\{a/t : t \in F_0,\ t \mid a\}\); for each uncovered \(n\) pick \(t_n\)
  with \(n t_n \in A\setminus\{a\}\), which exists because \(A \cap n\mathbb{N}\) is infinite when \(A\) is
  IP\*, and enlarge \(F_0\) by the finitely many \(t_n\)). *This one is the most delicate and is flagged for
  DISTILL.*

**`L-SPEC`(d) — the no-go.** *For every property \(\mathsf{L}\) listed in (b) and (c):*
\[
\mathcal{D} \in \mathsf{L} \iff \mathcal{D}\setminus\{2\} \in \mathsf{L}.
\]
*Hence **no theorem of the form "\(\mathcal{D}\) has largeness property \(\mathsf{L}\)" can imply
\(2 \in \mathcal{D}\)**: the hypothetical set \(\mathcal{D}\setminus\{2\}\) satisfies every such property
that \(\mathcal{D}\) does, and does not contain 2. The largeness program on \(\mathcal{D}\) — Pintz,
Banks, Goswami and any extension of it — **provably cannot reach the Twin Prime Conjecture.***

**Scope, stated precisely (this is where the claim must not overreach).** `L-SPEC`(d) excludes exactly
those conclusions that are *finitely stable predicates of \(\mathcal{D}\)*. It does **not** say that no
property of \(\mathcal{D}\) implies \(2 \in \mathcal{D}\) — the predicate "\(2 \in \mathcal{D}\)" is
itself a (non-finitely-stable) property, as is "\(\mathcal{D} = 2\mathbb{N}\)", and both trivially
suffice. The content is that the *entire standard largeness hierarchy* consists of finitely stable
predicates, and is therefore, as a class, incapable of the conclusion — however far it is extended.

## Part 4 — What is and is not load-bearing

**Load-bearing and self-contained.** `L-PIGEON` (i)–(iv) and `L-SPEC` (a)–(d) are proved above from
Hindman's theorem, Ramsey's theorem, and elementary combinatorics. **They do not depend on the
OPEN-capped citations.** If Banks's IP\* theorem or Goswami's multiplicative-syndeticity theorem were
withdrawn tomorrow, `L-SPEC` would be unaffected — it is a statement about *what such theorems can
imply*, not about whether they are true.

**Not load-bearing.** The Pintz/Banks/Goswami citations serve only to establish that the excluded program
is a **real, active** line rather than a straw man. The Pintz citation is verified at abstract level; the
other two are OPEN-capped at metadata level.

**Explicitly not claimed.** A22 is **not progress toward TPC**, **not a bound**, and **not a foundation**.
It touches C5 nowhere. It is a **wall on a different axis**, and under `AGENTS.md` a wall is a checkpoint,
never an exit.

    s=bytearray([1])*n; s[0:2]=b'\x00\x00'
    for i in range(2,int(n**0.5)+1):
        if s[i]: s[i*i::i]=bytearray(len(s[i*i::i]))
    return s
S=sieve(10**7)
def primerange(a,b): return [i for i in range(a,b) if S[i]]

def admissible(H):
    k=len(H)
    for p in primerange(2,k+2):
        if len(set(h%p for h in H))==p: return False

def shift_components(H,h):
    s=set(H); seen=set(); comps=[]
    for a in sorted(s):
        if a in seen or (a-h) in s: continue
        comp=[]; x=a
        while x in s: comp.append(x); seen.add(x); x+=h
        comps.append(comp)
    return comps
def alpha_shift(H,h): return sum((len(c)+1)//2 for c in shift_components(H,h))
