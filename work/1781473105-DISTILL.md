## ATTACK 1 — The equivalence: is SO-deavg a genuine iff, with the stated quantifiers?

`Σ_{n≤x} λ(n)λ(n+2) = o(x)` **⟺** *FULL every-scale local Fourier uniformity of `λ`

**The attack (search for the exact logical strength).** I searched for the literature's
*actual* equivalence between Fourier uniformity and Chowla. Surfaced verbatim:

> "The validity of the **logarithmic local t-Fourier uniformity conjecture for all t ≥ 1**
> is **equivalent to the logarithmic Chowla conjecture** on autocorrelations of the
> Liouville function and the logarithmically averaged version of Sarnak's conjecture."
> (search-surfaced, Tao 2015 blog / Liouville-function tag)

and the standard implication direction:

> "the standard argument exploits multiplicativity at small primes to **deduce Chowla-type
> conjectures from Fourier uniformity conjectures**." (search-surfaced)

**What the attack found — THREE precision gaps; the iff as written is NOT a theorem:**

1. **Log vs. Cesàro slippage (the decisive one).** The *established* equivalence is
   **logarithmic** Fourier uniformity ⟺ **logarithmic** Chowla. SO-deavg states the iff with
   **Cesàro** Chowla on the left and **non-averaged every-scale** Fourier uniformity on the
   right. That is a *strictly stronger, different* statement. Lifting the equivalence from
   the log world to the Cesàro/every-scale world is **exactly the open content** — it cannot
   be cited as established. The forward direction (every-scale degree-1 Fourier uniformity
   ⟹ Cesàro two-point Chowla) is *morally* the standard "multiplicativity at small primes"
   argument and is highly plausible, but I could not surface it stated for **Cesàro/every
   scale**; only for log averages. So even the ⟸ direction, as written, is **OPEN, not
   established.**

2. **Degree-1-alone vs. all-`t` bundling.** The clean literature iff is for **all `t ≥ 1`
   jointly** (it includes Sarnak). For the **two-point** correlation specifically, the
   relevant object is genuinely degree 1 (the pattern is CS-complexity 1 — concordant with
   L-C3-UNIF), so restricting to degree 1 for the *two-point* statement is defensible. But
   the *iff* the literature certifies is the bundled all-`t` one; the isolated "two-point
   Chowla ⟺ degree-1 every-scale Fourier uniformity" is **not** separately cited as an iff.
   The forward implication (Fourier-unif ⟹ Chowla) is the safe direction; the **converse**
   (two-point Chowla ⟹ degree-1 Fourier uniformity) is the part that is not obviously true
   in isolation and is not certified.

3. **Quantifier "every scale" is correctly the open endpoint** (this part survives): MRT did
   degree-1 Fourier uniformity **on average over `X`** for `H ≥ X^θ`; the open part is
   precisely *every* scale + *arbitrarily short* `H`. EXPLORE stated this correctly (see
   Attack 3).

**Verdict of Attack 1 (for EXPLORE to label).** The equivalence is **overclaimed as an
iff**. What is true: (a) the *log-averaged* version is an established iff (all `t`); (b) the
*forward* direction "every-scale Fourier uniformity ⟹ Cesàro two-point Chowla" is the
standard multiplicativity argument, plausible but **not surfaced as proven for Cesàro/every
