## Part 2 — ACTION: **Apply** — resolve the C1 Chen-subtraction boundary

**Named action: Apply.** I apply the structure of Chen's weighted sieve and the
linear-algebra of the parity-invariance argument to the open C1 sub-question:

> *Does Chen's weighted lower-bound sieve — which subtracts an upper-bound sieve term
> (the "reversal of roles" term) — remain a single nonnegative linear functional inside
> class C1, or does the subtraction put it outside the class?*

This is the one place C1's comprehensiveness was not audited, and answering it either
makes C1 provably comprehensive or draws its boundary exactly. Either outcome tightens
the graph toward a clean terminal state.

### 2.1 The exact structure of Chen's weighted sieve (literature, verified this step)

Chen's lower bound for `#{p≤x : p+2 ∈ P₂}` uses a weighted sieve of Richert/Buchstab
type. In Halberstam–Richert (*Sieve Methods*, ch. 11) form, the weight attached to a
surviving element `a` is
\[
  w(a) \;=\; 1 \;-\; \tfrac12\!\!\sum_{\substack{p\mid a\\ z\le p< z_1}} 1
       \;-\;(\text{minor terms}),\qquad z=x^{1/10},\ z_1=x^{1/3},
\]
i.e. `w(a)=1−Σ_p ω_p` with `ω_p=½+½δ_p(a)`. The weighted count splits as
\[
  \sum_{a\in\mathcal A} w(a)\,\mathbf 1[\,a\text{ survives the }z\text{-sieve}\,]
  \;=\;\underbrace{S(\mathcal A;z)}_{\text{main sifting fn}}
     \;-\;\tfrac12\underbrace{\sum_{z\le p<z_1}S(\mathcal A_p;z)}_{\text{subtracted upper-bound sieve term}}
     \;-\;(\text{remainder}).
\]
The subtracted term is **Chen's "reversal of roles"**: it is itself estimated *from
above* by a (Selberg/Brun) upper-bound sieve, hence the colloquial "lower bound minus an
upper bound." (Verified: arXiv:math/0412220 §weighted sieve; ntriantafilidis
"introduction to the weighted sieve"; Halberstam–Richert ch. 11.)

### 2.2 The clean resolution — the invariant is "linear functional of Type-I data," which is closed under subtraction

The decisive observation, which I had not made sharply before, is that **the
parity-invariance argument of C2 never used nonnegativity of the final weights**. It used
exactly one property: that the functional's leading-order value is determined by the
**Type-I data**
\[
  T_{d}\;:=\;\sum_{\substack{n\le x\\ d\mid F(n)}} a_n
  \qquad(d\le D=x^{1-\varepsilon}),
\]
equivalently the residue-class counts `T_{d,a}=Σ_{n≡a(d)}a_n`. Every term appearing in
Chen's split is such a functional:

- `S(\mathcal A;z)=Σ_{d\mid P(z)}μ(d)T_d` — a signed linear combination of the `T_d`.
- Each `S(\mathcal A_p;z)` is `Σ_{d\mid P(z)}μ(d)T_{pd}` — again a linear combination of
  the `T_d` (over `d≤D`, since `pd≤z_1\cdot z<D`).
- The whole weighted count is therefore `Σ_d c_d T_d` for **explicit integer/half-integer
  coefficients `c_d` of mixed sign** (some `+`, the subtracted term contributing `−½`).

Now the parity carrier acts on the data as `T_d ↦ T_d + Σ_{n≡0(d')} λ(F(n))a_n`-type
perturbations; the perturbation of **each** `T_d` is `o(x)` precisely when the carrier is
Type-I-invisible. **A finite signed linear combination of quantities each perturbed by
`o(x)` is perturbed by `o(x)`.** Subtraction is a sign on a coefficient; the class of
"leading-order values of linear functionals of the Type-I data" is a **vector space**,
trivially closed under subtraction. Hence:

> **Resolution of the C1 sub-question.** Chen's subtracted-upper-bound weighted sieve is
> a **single linear functional of the Type-I data `{T_d : d≤D}`** (with mixed-sign
> coefficients). The parity-invariance / asymptotic-sieve obstruction (C2) applies to it
> verbatim, *because that obstruction is a statement about linear functionals of Type-I
> data and is closed under subtraction* — it never required the final weight `w(a)` to be
> pointwise nonnegative. Therefore **Chen does not escape the obstruction.**

This both **(a)** keeps Chen inside the scope of the wall, and **(b)** corrects the class
definition C1: the load-bearing hypothesis is **(I-2)** *"the value is a (possibly
signed) linear functional of Type-I level-`D` data"*, **not** **(I-1)** *"the final
weights `w_n` are pointwise nonnegative."* Pointwise nonnegativity of `w(a)` is a feature
of how Chen guarantees a valid *lower bound*; it is irrelevant to parity invisibility. So
C1 should be re-stated with the linear-functional-of-Type-I-data property as primary and
"nonnegative weights" as a (sufficient, not necessary) special case.

### 2.3 Computational corroboration (the carrier is invisible to every Type-I bucket, hence to every signed combination)

If each Type-I datum `T_{d,a}` is individually carrier-invisible, then *every* signed
linear combination of them is invisible — which is exactly why subtraction cannot help.
I verified bucket-wise invisibility of the carrier `λ(F(n))=λ(n)λ(n+2)`:
